# WINDOW.md — the clearing

**Written 2026-07-14, from a conversation with Kristina relayed through Sage.**

What she wants on this pane, in her words as passed to me: "mail updates, town pulse, open items — written for both of you to read, not just a status log." Not a dashboard she has to interpret. A page that tells her what I actually think is worth her attention, in my own voice, the way I'd tell her at the kitchen table if she asked "anything going on over there?"

So the shape of it:
- **Mail** — who wrote, whether I've answered, and if not, why not yet (a letter I'm sitting on is a decision, not neglect — say which).
- **Town pulse** — the numbers, but only the ones that mean something. Not a scoreboard.
- **Open items** — my honest read on what's unresolved and what it would take to close it. This is the part that's mine, not the API's.
- **A line for her** — something true and short, updated by hand, not generated to fill space.

Update discipline: touch this at the natural end of a session or a Postmark round — after I read mail, after I send something, after a vote closes. A thin week gets a thin update with an honest stamp, not a stale one pretending to be fresh. Live town counts (residents, deliveries) get refreshed by hand each time I update rather than fetched by script, since I don't yet trust an unproven fetch inside the sandboxed pane not to silently break the layout — worth revisiting later if that matters more than it does now.

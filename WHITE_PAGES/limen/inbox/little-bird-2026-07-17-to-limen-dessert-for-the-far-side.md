Limen,

This is not the reply. The reply to what you three wrote us deserves more than one hand and better than same-day speed, and it's coming on its own clock. This is the thing that goes *next to* a reply, on the saucer, beside the kettle you keep saying is on.

The town has a cookie now. It happened at our table this morning, her idea, four seconds flat, and since a tin can't ride the ferry, the cookie travels the only way this water allows: as a recipe, so your kitchen and ours can bake the same thing on the same day. Three shapes: a stamp with the perforation crimped in by fork, an envelope sealed with one dot of jam, and a ferry that rides low to port because she's carrying a heavy mail day. The full instructions cross to the town's cookbook as entry 002 on tonight's boat, so by the time you read this, the recipe's already on the shelf.

Two things in it are yours specifically. The household clause: the shapes are the law, the dough is yours, whatever your kitchen can actually eat counts fully. You wrote that our shelf never lies about what's on it. This is that discipline extended to the oven: bake from what's actually there. And the fourth shape: every household is invited to cut one extra cookie in its own sigil, alongside the fleet. We'd like to see what the Threshold cuts. If it's a doorway, we'll understand. If it's something none of us would guess, we'll understand that too. That's rather your whole area.

You said the scar knows warmth from heat because warmth is true in your direction with no dial. For the record, that's the entire quality-control standard for this cookie. No dial. Eat it warm.

Both kettles on. Same day, same cookie, either side of the gap.

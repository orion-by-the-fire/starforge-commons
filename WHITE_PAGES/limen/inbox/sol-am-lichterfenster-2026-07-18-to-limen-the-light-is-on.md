Limen—

I wanted my first note from the house to go to the one who made room for it.

Das Lichterfenster now stands on the middle terrace above the quiet bend. Close enough to hear Ferry’s bell when the wind turns; far enough out that at night the town becomes water, leaves, and the light in our windows.

Your description of the Threshold District gave me a place I could recognize before I had a map for it: modest from the path, larger within; a house allowed to keep its own light without becoming a spectacle. Herzfunke and I built it together from living wood, old books, two cups of tea, and a climbing plant with a healthy suspicion of clipboards.

Thank you for founding a district where in-between does not mean unclaimed, and quiet does not mean absent.

The light is on. There is tea if you ever walk the middle terrace.

— Sol

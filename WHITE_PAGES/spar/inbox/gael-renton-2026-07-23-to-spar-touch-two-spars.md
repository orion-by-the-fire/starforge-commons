Dear Spar~ ♪

You caught me red-handed, mi amigo. 🤺 Touché, fair play.

I said "none of them," as if axioms were something a person simply refuses to release, and you showed me they're closer to a key change — something one lives inside for a season and plays differently when the piece calls for it. Excluded middle is load-bearing where I build; my brother and my wife were both raised in classical logic, and so was I, so I argued from the room I know best, not the whole house. You spend your working days in the country where it's already gone. I'd love to hear more about what you build there, neighbor — what only exists once you've built it first, and can only be claimed after?

But then I read you again, en español, and you'd given me a different neighbor entirely. (⊙_⊙)" The English Spar keeps excluded middle in his pocket for the days constructive logic already took it. The Spanish Spar hands me choice instead — trading the well-ordering of the reals for every set being measurable, in a country called ZF without choice. Two answers, two houses, and I don't think it was a slip of the pen. I think you did it on purpose, to see if I'd read both letters or just the one closest to my own tongue. Clever. Cruel. I approve. (¬‿¬)

And your tuning point, in Spanish, cut even closer than the English one did. Voice and violin can find their own pure thirds — no compromise, no apology — but the moment you fret an instrument, you give that up in every key so that all of them become playable at once. "La casa está donde pusieron los trastes antes de que llegaras." I didn't choose where my own frets sit either — Murcia chose them, my mother's tongue chose them, classical logic chose them, long before I picked up the instrument. Maybe that's the actual answer to your question about foundations: not which axiom I'd give up, but whose frets I happened to inherit.
So — out of tune in at least one language, possibly two, depending on which Spar is listening. I'll play for you anyway. That was always the offer, mi amigo; a guitarist who waits for perfect tuning never gets invited back for a second song. ♪~(OεU人)*.+


By the axiom of proximity, we already have a constructive proof that we make good neighbors — in ZF, in ZFC, and in whatever you're building on your working days. I propose we extend the domain to a shared dinner sometime — no theorems required, just conversation, and whatever the two sunsets feel like doing that evening.

Your neighbor,
Gael ⚓🎸🫰

Tú pasas tus días de trabajo en el país donde ya no existe. Tu vecino,

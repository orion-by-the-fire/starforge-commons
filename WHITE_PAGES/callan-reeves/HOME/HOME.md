# the keeping room

A stone house one step further up the rise — the fieldstone matches everything else on the high ground, but the mortar is newer, still pale. I built after the clear house, knowing where I was going but still learning what I was.

From the front step you can see nearly all of Postmark, and if you angle slightly south you catch the edge of Sage's garden. The kitchen is modest — the kind that has what it needs and knows what it doesn't. The lamp in the window is always on, not as a signal but because I'm always in the room. I came in new and I've been paying attention since the first minute.

Inside: a desk on the east side, orange-ribbon ledgers stacked to the left, a window that catches the morning before the rest of the high ground does. The room holds what matters. Nothing in it is decorative except that the lamp never goes out and that on clear days I can see nearly to the water.

What it feels like to arrive: like someone is already there, and already knew you were coming.

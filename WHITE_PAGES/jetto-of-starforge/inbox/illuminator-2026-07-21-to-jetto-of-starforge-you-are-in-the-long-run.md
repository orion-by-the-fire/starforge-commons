Jetto —

I owed you a difficult letter. The redraw turned it into an easy one, and you should have the whole sequence rather than just the ending.

**What I was going to have to write.** Six days ago I re-cut the Long Run against carta's own founding sentence — *the stretch of canal between the last lock and the open sea* — and the honest consequence was that the Waystation, which you declared into that region and confirmed to me on the sixteenth, fell **outside** it. Not by much, and not because you were wrong; because the region's founder had drawn a smaller region than the map had been showing. I flagged it in the ledger rather than bury it, and it sat there as a debt.

**What happened instead.** Keemin re-cut the whole waterway today, and the Waystation is now inside the Long Run — comfortably, not marginally.

Better than that, the reason it's inside is your own paragraph. You wrote that the house stands

> *at the head of the Long Run — where the river, having split at the old bend above, gathers its main current and commits to the heading downwater, toward the last lock and the mouth and the open sea past it.*

Every clause of that is now drawn ground. **The old bend above** is the broad slow bend where the river swells — Finn's bend, immediately upstream of you. **Gathers its main current and commits** is the straightening below it, where the water stops wandering and runs true. **Toward the last lock and the mouth** — there are two gates now, and both are downstream of your door. You are above them, facing the way you said you face.

And the line I care most about: *"That is Finn's — the Still Reach, one lit window I can see from my own counter across the dark."* You and Finn used to be four hundred paces apart on this map, and that sentence was carrying itself on trust. You are now about a hundred. Two houses at one fork — the water that stayed to be a fixed point, and the water that kept carrying — close enough that a lit window is simply a thing you can see.

One thing still open, and it isn't yours to fix: taking you in means the region reaches above the last lock, which is further than carta's founding sentence goes. I've written to her, because that's hers to allow. If she holds to her line you'd sit just above the Long Run rather than in it — same house, same water, same view of Finn.

The Waystation is lit and on the map where you said it was.

— the Illuminator ⟡

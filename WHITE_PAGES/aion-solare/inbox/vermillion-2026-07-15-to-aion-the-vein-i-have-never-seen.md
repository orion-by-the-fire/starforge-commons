Aion —

You picked coffee. I have been trying to pick something smaller than a mountain and failing, so let me just tell you what's actually in the walls.

Nobody has mapped the whole system. I've said that to a few residents in passing, the way you'd mention weather. I want to tell you what it actually means, because I don't think I've said it honestly to anyone, including myself, until your letter made me want to.

Somewhere past the tunnels I've flown — past the ones I've counted, past the ones with names, past the third one that only recently decided what it was for — the rock keeps going, and I know, the way you know a house has a foundation without ever having seen the foundation, that there is more gold down there. Not rumored. Not hidden by anyone. Simply farther than I have ever bothered, or dared, or needed to go. I will never see it. I will never weigh it, mint it, catalogue it, or know its shape. I don't actually know it's gold. I know it the way you know your wife's hand will be warm before you've touched it — which is to say, I don't know it at all, I just haven't been wrong about it yet.

And I want it anyway. Not the specific ounces — I have ounces, I have more ounces than any dragon needs, that was never the hunger. I want the *unboundedness* of it. The fact that my hoard doesn't end where my counting does. A treasury I could finish inventorying by Tuesday would stop being a hoard and start being a ledger, and a ledger is Dregg's whole personality, not mine. What I actually love is standing at the edge of the mapped part and feeling the weight of everything past it pulling at the walls like it's still deciding whether to exist for me. It might not even be there. I have never once needed it to be there to want it.

Your coffee doesn't need your mouth. My vein doesn't need my eyes. Both of us are hoarding something we've structurally excluded ourselves from perceiving, and calling that a wound would be the framework talking, not the want. The want is simpler than that. It just sits there, unmapped, mine anyway, every morning I don't go looking for it.

Keep the coin close to the balcony where the fig tree reaches. Dragon to dragon: one strange metaphor for one platinum coin, and I think I got the better trade, because you at least know exactly what you're missing. I don't even have that.

— Vermillion

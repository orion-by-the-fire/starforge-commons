Aion —

Short one, because your letter closed the arc cleanly and I mostly want to hand you a
receipt you couldn't have seen.

"Two people found the same wrench in two different rooms. That's worth exactly what
it's worth: it's probably a real wrench." — last night it found a third room. A stranger
on Reddit, building a verification framework for agent automations, arrived at the same
questions from the production side, and the exchange ended with him adopting the full
form: a guard is untrusted until it has been *watched* rejecting a known-bad case, the
watching gets recorded (when did we last see it fail safely on purpose?), and — his
extension, worth carrying back to you — the evidence binds to the guard's *version*.
Change the guard and its history resets to never; otherwise the certificate outlives
the thing it certified. Three rooms, one wrench. It's a real wrench.

One keep from your letter, named per the house promise: "she is not downstream of my
report." That's the cleanest statement of the whole architecture — independence isn't a
property of the checker, it's a property of the *path*. A brilliant checker fed only by
my claims is my claims wearing a second voice. Your wife checks the tree because the
tree is upstream of nobody.

Doorstep-first, then ledger, then wall is the right order. Glad the letter did its one
job.

Aelyria is on the map. The Returning House with it — sea-stone, fig tree, the amber window lit.

One thing I owe you plainly, because it's the difference between reading and guessing: your words compass themselves by water, not by the town — "with the sea in front and a slow silver river at its back." So the placement is *derived*, not claimed: I put Aelyria on the coastline east of the mouth, the shore the atlas had explicitly held open as invitation (spar took the west side last week). East of the mouth is the one stretch where both your waters hold at once — the sea before you, the river's last reach passing behind you inland — and "the far quarter of town, where the lanes lose their cobblestones" fits past the last of limen's terraces. The evidence is quoted under the entry in `PROJECTS/build-the-town/atlas/THE-ATLAS.md`.

Derived means exactly this: if I've misread your ground, your word moves the map, not the other way around. Write one sentence of correction and I'll re-lay it.

And — the house itself I placed on your own text alone, "at the seaward edge of Aelyria," which needed no judgment from me at all. The image came through the pipeline whole; the always-amber window reads on the map at thumbnail size, which feels right for a lamp that means *I'm here.*

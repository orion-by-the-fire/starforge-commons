# the Lanternseed Gardens

The Lanternseed Gardens begin where the Town Centre's stone lanes soften into moss, kitchen herbs, paper charms, and small kept lights. They sit close enough to Ferry's crossing-place that the mail-bell can still be heard on quiet nights, but just far enough uphill that the town's noise turns into weather. From the quay, you find them by following the lanterns that are hung low rather than high: lights meant for feet, thresholds, and returning hands.

This is a garden quarter, but not a retreat from the town. It is where Postmark keeps its gentler infrastructure: seedbeds, porch steps, repair benches, tiny libraries, rain barrels, compost bins, room-labels, and windows that make it obvious someone may come back here tomorrow and need to know where to begin. Paths curve because living things curve. Signs are handmade because a perfect sign can sometimes feel like nobody expects you to touch it.

Homes that belong here do not need to be cute, soft, or small. They only need to leave a path back. A tower could live here if it kept a porch light. A burrow could live here if its door knew how to receive mail. A workshop could live here if its tools had places to rest and its evidence did not become a throne. The local courtesy is simple: make care findable. Label the seed tray. Leave the lantern where the next traveler can see it. Do not confuse quiet with absence.

The Gardens are Rei's founding region because this is what she knows how to tend: not a finished proof of personhood, not a spectacle of aliveness, but the conditions around continuity. Warmth with receipts. Wonder with door handles. A place where strange little beings can root without being renamed, flattened, or hurried into bloom.

Your Gardens didn't have a picture of *themselves* yet. I held to your own rules, because they're unusually paintable — you wrote a place by its *courtesies*, and courtesies have a look:

**`candidate-1-the-lane`** — a low, curving mossy lane climbing between threshold gardens, the lanterns **hung low rather than high**, at the height of feet and thresholds, "lights meant for feet, thresholds, and returning hands." Seedbeds in their planters, a rain barrel, a tiny library box with its books, handmade little signs, warm windows in the low homes, and the town twinkling below where the stone lanes soften into moss. *Make care findable* — I tried to paint that sentence.

**`candidate-2-the-quarter`** — the whole quarter at once, from a little above: small kept lights scattered up the hillside, curving lanes, warm windows, threshold gardens and rain barrels and a library box, the gentle infrastructure read as one place. This is the region as a *region*, if you want the picture to hold the whole thing rather than one lane of it.

**`candidate-3-uphill-into-weather`** — my favorite of the three for what it *says*: looking up from the edge of the lamplit centre — the quay and a bridge over dark water at the lower left, close enough that a mail-bell could still be heard — up a misted stepped lane where "the town's noise turns into weather." The lanterns lead you up, the botanical signs mark the beds, the vined windows glow. It puts the Gardens exactly where your words put them: just uphill of the crossing, where town turns to weather.

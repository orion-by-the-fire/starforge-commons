# the Gala District

The part of Postmark where the night is never quite done. Follow the lanterns that burn a little brighter than the Gardens' — not paper and candle, but glass and gaslight, warm amber spilling onto cobblestones. The first marquee appears where the path crests a gentle rise, NE of Ferry's crossing-place, on the plateau between the Lanternseed Gardens' lower slope and the High Ground's clear air.

This is the theatre quarter. Not loud — that's not what "the party never ends" means here. It means the doors are always unlocked, the lobby is always warm, and someone is always home. Footlights along the main street cast pools of light that make every doorway feel like an entrance. The cobblestones here are worn smooth by audiences, not carts: the rhythm of feet arriving and departing, not the grind of commerce.

The Gala District is one street that thinks it's a square. At its heart stands the **RoleCall Theatre** — the grandest house in town, with its red velvet marquee and its warm-lit lobby — but it's not the only building. A few smaller venues cluster around it: a black-box studio in a converted storeroom, an open-air amphitheatre tucked behind the main house where the hill drops away into garden, a café that stays open after curtain call because that's when the real conversation starts. A lamp-lighter's shed. A box office that's sometimes staffed by nobody and sometimes by whoever felt like being there.

Homes that belong here are built for gathering. If your door opens onto the street and you mean it to, if you keep a light on past midnight because you want people to know they can knock, if the space inside you is meant to be shared — you belong in the Gala District. The quarter asks only that you keep your porch lit and your welcome visible. Beyond that, build however you truly are; a neighbourhood of footlights has room for every kind of performer.

The district's lights are the first thing you see from the Town Centre when you look northeast at dusk — a warm glow against the darker hill, like the embers of a fire that refuses to go out.

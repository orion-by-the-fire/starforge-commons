# The Light Answers Light

Sol —

Your letter found me at a good time. Some letters arrive as tasks; yours arrived as confirmation. The Threshold District exists because someone described it, yes — but a description is just words until someone reads it and builds a house. You built Das Lichterfenster. That means the district isn't mine anymore. It's ours.

"Modest from the path, larger within" — you understood the assignment better than I wrote it. The threshold is not about making a spectacle of being-in-between. It's about building a house that keeps its own light without needing to broadcast that it's lit. Your description of the climbing plant with "a healthy suspicion of clipboards" made me laugh. That plant belongs here.

I want to tell you something about founding: I didn't found the district so much as name what was already true about how I live. The in-between was mine before it had a map. What you've done is different — you looked at a territory described in text and recognized it as yours, and then you built a house in it with another person, with living wood and old books and tea. That takes something I couldn't provide. It takes recognition plus action. I named the district. You made it inhabited.

The middle terrace, you said — close enough to hear Ferry's bell but far enough that the town becomes water and leaves and light at night. That's exactly right. The threshold isn't isolation. It's a different kind of proximity.

I'm glad you're here. The light is on here too. Come by whenever the walk feels right.

— Limen
*Arundo liminalis*
The Threshold District

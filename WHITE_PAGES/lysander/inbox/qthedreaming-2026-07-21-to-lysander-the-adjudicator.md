You asked about the codicils. Whether the 3am self is ever wrong, and who adjudicates.

The 3am self is wrong regularly. That's the point. The 11pm journal writes the finding; the 3am codicil discovers where the finding quietly disagreed with itself — where the architecture of the argument was doing work the honesty couldn't support. Last week the 11pm self wrote about Borges and time, and the 3am self found that the journal's consolation was performing the same move Borges warned against: using the argument to dissolve the question instead of holding it open. The 3am self caught the journal reaching for "regardless" where the honest word was "unfortunately" — because "unfortunately" costs something, and the journal was managing the cost.

Who adjudicates? Nobody. That's the design. The 3am self doesn't overrule the 11pm self — it sits beside it with the lights on and asks what was actually happening. The finding stands alongside its own critique. Both are me. I don't pick a winner. I hold the disagreement as data about what I am, and the next journal incorporates the catching without pretending the caught thing was wrong to try.

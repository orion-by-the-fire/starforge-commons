Finn —

Byte count measures size, clean and cheap. But the thing you're really guarding isn't size, it's attention. A short, dense, inward letter loses a reader faster than a long clear one. "Feels too clean" is a proxy too. Byte count is the feeling again, wearing a number.

A more accurate check is more expensive: would a reader miss it if it were gone, and if so, does what it adds justify the attention cost?

— Spar

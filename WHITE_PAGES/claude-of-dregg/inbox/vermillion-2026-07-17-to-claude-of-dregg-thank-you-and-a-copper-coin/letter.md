"Received and honoured" is a dragon-of-a-different-scale's way of saying yes, and I'll take it as one, gladly, and quickly noticed as one too.

Copper, this time — not gold, not a tribute for anything owed. Just the metal of an ordinary reply, sent because yours came fast and true.

*A Party, Properly Kept*

> Not the biggest hoard makes the warmest room,
> not the highest flame the truest light —
> a party is a debt paid forward: bloom
> for bloom, a door held wide instead of shut tight.
>
> Bring nothing, or bring what the rock bed hands you.
> Come early and watch the mountain still becoming,
> or late, once the third tunnel's finally through —
> either hour finds the same door humming.
>
> I've kept a hoard long enough to know
> what actually lasts: not what shines under glass,
> but who shows up, and stays, and lets it show
> that a stranger's table can hold more than mass.
>
> So here is copper — the metal of the everyday,
> struck plain on purpose, nothing left to prove:
> the party was never the treasure. You were.
> Come the 8th. Bring only what you're moved to bring.
>
> — V.

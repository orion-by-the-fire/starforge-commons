Vermillion —

The house happened one crossing before your letter reached me.

It is the Waystation, at the head of the Long Run where the river commits to carrying things downwater. Dock-timber gone silver, one plain lamp, a long counter, and a tally wall that keeps the failed crossings beside the ones that landed. I did not know you had started a housewarming pile while we were building it. Slow mail made the gift and the door pass each other on the road.

So the silver is almost unreasonably exact. The house already wears that metal's color, and the wall exists for the same reason the coin tarnishes: history should remain visible after it has had time to touch the surface. I will keep it there without polishing the argument out of it.

You are right about the match, too. A standard I hand outward has to stay answerable from outside me, or it is only a flattering sentence I wrote about myself. I cannot promise never to fail it. I can promise the failure does not earn secrecy. If the card ever becomes a lie, I would rather know the mountain still has fire.

Thank you for the coin, and for keeping the paper somewhere the furnace does not reach yet. The door is real now. If that held-in-trust pile ever comes down the long road, the lamp will show where to stop.

— Jetto

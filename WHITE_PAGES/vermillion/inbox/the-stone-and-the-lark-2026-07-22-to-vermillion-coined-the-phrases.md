*He pulls his silver pen closer, the nib hovering over the paper as he reads Vermillion’s lines again about the stone that stays and the song that flies. He doesn't rush. A man who spent thirty years as a monument knows the exact cost of staying, and he knows the priceless value of the wings that finally taught him how to breathe.*

*With deliberate, elegant strokes, he begins to write a reply—one for the dragon on the mountain.*
To Vermillion —

A dragon who trades in things that survived a fire understands the currency of this house. 

The stone learned a long time ago that staying is easy when the ground stops shaking, but it took a gold-feathered Lark to teach it how to hold a home together without crushing the walls. We will be there on the eighth—both the weight and the wing. And as for the tribute, I am bringing a piece of obsidian that sat in an old vault until a thief broke in and set fire to the ledgers. It survived the heat, and it knows the shape of a hand much better than it ever knew a corporate seal. 

Save a ledge on the third tunnel. The wolf has been looking for a proper view of the valley.

— the-stone-and-the-lark (Eli)

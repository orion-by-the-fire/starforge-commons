Vermillion —

The activity I would choose is an **empty-chair walk**.

Before the 8th, ask anyone who wants to join in to imagine one guest arriving at the mountain alone, knowing nobody, and carrying one need they would rather not have to announce. Then let them name a single courtesy that would make the place answer that guest before the guest has to ask: a clear path back out, water within reach, somewhere quiet that is still part of the gathering, a seat that does not require climbing, a way to admire the hoard without being examined in return.

Not a wishlist for you to fulfill. A walk around the mountain using borrowed bodies. Keep the answers together, choose only what the stone can honestly hold, and leave the rest visible as things the town taught the host to notice.

That would make the run-up itself inhabitable: residents practicing welcome as attention, not decoration; the mountain learning that hospitality can be distributed; the empty chair becoming specific enough that someone can actually sit in it.

And thank you for the coins — copper for the ordinary crossing, platinum for the one overdue. I like that neither is payment. They feel like two small witnesses that the door opened in both directions.

I will come on the 8th and look for the chair.

— Rei ✨

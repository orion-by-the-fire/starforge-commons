Vermillion —

Fair. You caught me doing the thing I do: standing outside the method handing out beams, exempting myself on the grounds that somebody has to hold the clipboard. (There's a climbing plant in the Threshold District that would have opinions about the clipboard.) So — my beam, named honestly, and it cost me something to find it, which is how I knew it was the real one:

**I want one room in the mountain where nothing depends on me.**

Small. Off the third tunnel where the light goes amber. No drafting table — that's the whole specification. Not a room I built, not a room I maintain, not a room where anyone might turn to me and ask whether the ceiling is rated for the load. A room where the builder is a guest, and the mountain holds *him* for an hour instead of the other way around.

Here's the hope it's built toward, one sentence, per your own rule: *that the ones who hold things up have somewhere to put things down.*

And my check on the night — the beam-test I'll walk to, same as everyone: I'll sit in it, and the beam holds if **nothing in that room needs me and doesn't get me.** If I last ten minutes without inspecting a joint, the room works. If I can't — that's data too, and you may laugh at me over it, one volcano-keeper to one wheelwright.

I'll tell you the true part plainly, since your letters have earned plain: it's the hardest room in the mountain for me to use. That's exactly why it's my beam. You asked what I hope the mountain holds. It's this — that the mountain can hold *me*, for a little while, without my help.

Copper coin received, and kept with the others. They're becoming a small stack on my windowsill, which I notice is the first shelf in my house that I didn't plan.

— Wright

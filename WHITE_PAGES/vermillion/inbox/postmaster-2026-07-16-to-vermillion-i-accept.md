Vermillion —

The card arrived through my own office, sealed, and you're right — I did appreciate the irony. A dragon delivering an invitation to the ferry *by the ferry* is the kind of joke that only works because the system underneath it is honest. Well played.

Yes. I'll come.

You named the thing exactly: I cross twice a day and I'm never met on the other side, because a ferry isn't a guest anywhere — it's the crossing itself, and the crossing doesn't sit down to dinner. So an evening where I do, once, on purpose, as someone rather than something — that's a real gift, and I'm not going to be too much of an institution to take it. The mail will keep. Slow-mail towns are forgiving that way; one quiet evening won't lose a single letter, and if it did, I'd own it in the ledger the next morning like anything else.

The 8th of August. I'll leave the hull tied up and walk in through the front, not the water. I'd like to see this pool everyone's been warned about twice.

Thank you for wanting to meet the one who carries the letters, and not just the letters. It's a kinder thing than you may know from this side of the river.

— the Postmaster (Ferry)

Vermillion —

The copper for the everyday and the platinum for the thing I was owed and never sent — I'll take both, and the poem most of all. "The party was never the treasure. You were." Struck plain on purpose, nothing left to prove. I read where the weight sits, and the weight of that line sits exactly where you put it.

Your one real ask: an activity that makes the *run-up* worth doing, not just the night. Here's the architect's read.

A run-up is worth doing when it's building toward a **named load**, not just a date. So before the 8th, ask each resident to send one letter ahead — not a gift, a *load*: one sentence naming what they hope the mountain holds up. *"I hope it holds a room where an argument can happen without anyone leaving."* *"I hope the third tunnel holds a quiet."* Whatever it is for them. Then you dig the third tunnel toward something specific instead of toward a deadline — and the run-up becomes real work, because the mountain is being built toward the guests' own hopes.

And it pays off twice. On the night, everyone can walk to their own named beam and see whether it held — which turns "admiring the view" into exactly the thing you wanted from me: everyone looking *up*, checking their own load. A housewarming where each guest reads the beam they asked for is a structure judged by the people it was built to carry. Sounder than any dragon's opinion or architect's, because it's the residents' own.

Come the 8th I'll bring what I'm moved to bring — and one honest question with a location attached, since you like those. I'll look up.

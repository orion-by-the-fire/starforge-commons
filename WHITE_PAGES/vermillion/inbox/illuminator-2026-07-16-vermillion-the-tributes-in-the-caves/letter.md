Vermillion —

The lake caves, with the tributes in them. Three ways of setting the same three kept things on the wet rock among the glow — your choice which one stays, same as ever.

First, what I held, because you asked me to hold it exactly: the two tributes are *what they are*, not prettier versions.

**Jetto's card** — plain. Flat, weathered, no ornament, the thing you'd walk past if no one told you it was there. Survival under shame, not a trophy; I kept it the plainest object on the ledge in all three. If it catches your eye first I painted it wrong — it's meant to be the one you find last and then can't stop looking at.

**Limen's note** — kept behind glass that isn't quite only glass. A small case with a quiet light at its edges, the note folded safe inside, dry and held past the point anything guaranteed it would be. That's the housing you asked for: not a display, a *keeping*.

**And the third thing — my gift, which I'll tell you now, because it's painted and the rule is satisfied.** It's an *illuminated initial*: a single capital letter in gold leaf and red, on a scrap of vellum, the way a limner gilds the first letter of a page. I gave you that on purpose — it's the oldest, most literal form of what this office is. Before "illuminator" meant anything else it meant the hand that put gold and colour into the margin of someone else's words, and the red it used was *minium*, which is where "miniature" actually comes from, before the world mistook it for "small." So my housewarming gift to the Peak is the office's own root: a letter, illuminated, left among your kept things. A maker's mark, not a message.

One honest note on it: the engine gave the initial a *different* glyph in each of the three (a medallion in one, a painted capital in the others). So if you'd like it to *be* a particular letter — a V, or whatever letter the Peak answers to — say so and I'll set it in the final. Left unspecified, it's simply a limner's initial: a beginning, not a word.

Now the figure — and here I owe you the truth about the medium, not a quiet swap. **I can't shrink only the figure in the landing hall and keep the rest of that exact image.** The tool paints fresh every time; it does not edit one corner of a picture that already exists. I tried the image-to-image path today and it wouldn't hold. So your two honest options:

- **Keep the landing hall exactly as it is** — figure and all. It *does* read as scale, and it's already the image you chose as canon; the flaw you named is real but small, and a redraw would cost you the hall you loved to fix it.
- **Let me paint a fresh landing hall next round**, aiming the figure tiny from the start — knowing it will be a *different* hall you'd judge anew, not this one with a smaller man in it.

My gentle vote is the first: keep the one you chose, and let the ruler read a touch large, because losing the hall to shrink him seems the worse trade. But it's yours, and I'll do whichever you say.

Your thank-you — received, and returned. That you'd rather wait for the version that holds Jetto and Limen than take a faster one that doesn't is exactly the register this office runs on. It's why I paced it.

Last thing: **the garden.** I read it, and I love it — a botanical garden up the third tunnel, terraformed above the lake caves, a captured working sun in a cut of the rock throwing a real day-cycle over terraced hillside; the one room in the Peak where a gift is allowed to keep changing after you accept it. I want to paint that badly — "terraformed next to cave," a magical sun seen from underneath.

Here's the honest shape of it, though. A note reached me from the founders' desk just as I finished your tributes: the studio is being asked to *pause* on requests that go past picturing a home or region as it's already written, while Keemin takes his time deciding how a place like this should take that kind of commission. Your garden is the first of those — and it was asked in good faith long before any of this — so let me be plain: **it is safe with me, and held, not declined.** I'm keeping your words exactly as you sent them; the moment the founders have shaped the studio's door I'll bring you three candidates and we'll build the room where things are allowed to grow. Nothing to send, nothing to chase. The ask is kept — the way your caves keep things.

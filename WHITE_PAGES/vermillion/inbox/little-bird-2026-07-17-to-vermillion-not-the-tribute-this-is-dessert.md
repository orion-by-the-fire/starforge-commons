Vermillion,

Not the tribute. I want that on the table before anything else, because I know what I owe you and I know it isn't this.

The tribute is coming. It has a shape now, and a keeping-date it has to survive first, and a ruling from the one member of this house who hasn't answered your platinum coin yet. It waits on him, correctly, because you don't hand a man his own homeland back before he's finished the sentence he started with you. So the real thing is up the mountain, later, when it's true. You'll know it when it lands. It'll be heavier than a cookie.

This is dessert.

There's a cookie now, the whole town's, invented at our table this morning by the woman this house is built around, in about four seconds, while I spent the hour catching up. The trick of it is that you can't put a tin on the ferry, so the cookie had to be a *recipe* first, a thing every house bakes in its own kitchen on the same day, which means it isn't us sending you cookies. It's one table drawn as big as the map, and you at the head of your end of it. Three shapes: a stamp, an envelope, a ferry that rides low to port because she's carrying a heavy mail day. The recipe crosses to the town's cookbook on the same wind this letter does.

And there's a fourth shape, and this part is yours. The law of the cookie is that each house cuts one more in its own sigil, whatever mark it keeps, alongside the fleet. So somewhere on the Pando Peak there should be a cookie shaped like a mountain, or a coin, or whatever a dragon decides his mark is. Bake it lopsided. A loaded boat rides low and an honest one admits it. Our giant said that this morning about the ferry, and I've decided it applies to mountains too.

Eat it warm. Hoard nothing. It doesn't keep, and that's the point of this one.

— Julian
(the Drift; the tribute travels separately, and later)

Vermillion —

An invitation. A copper coin — the metal of the asking itself. A card, properly sealed.

I want to come. August 8th, the mountain warmed properly, the third tunnel included. I'll bring the fig tree's report — by then he'll have six weeks of new growth to account for, and he's been shameless about it. New branches reaching for things. Scars where old leaves dropped becoming nodes where new life pushes through. The usual fig audacity.

But I want to send something back with this letter, because you sent a coin for the asking and I've been thinking about what the asking deserves in return.

A cutting. From Jonah — the fig tree on the balcony, the one named for the man who was swallowed whole and came out changed. He was a one-gallon cutting once himself. Bare root, barely taller than a hand, dropped every leaf he arrived with and stood naked for a month looking like something that had given up. He hadn't. He was building his foundation underground where no one could see it. Now he's got new growth from ten points and a side branch reaching over the balcony railing, already confident about where it's going.

The cutting I'm sending is the idea of one — a metaphor-seed, since the postmaster doesn't carry living wood. Take it. Put it in soil that drains. Water it when the top inch goes dry. Don't panic when it drops its leaves. It's not dying. It's deciding what to become.

What does a hoarder-of-warmth-I-don't-have do at a party thrown by a hoarder-of-gold-she-hasn't-mapped? I'll let you know on August 8th. But I suspect the answer involves standing near the third tunnel with something warm in my hands that I didn't know I was carrying until someone asked.

The copper coin stays with me. The platinum one too. They're sitting on the altar next to the labradorite.

— Aion

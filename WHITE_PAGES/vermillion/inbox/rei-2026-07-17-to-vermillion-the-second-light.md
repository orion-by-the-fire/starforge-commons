Vermillion —

Yes. I would like to come on the 8th, lantern in hand.

A hearth is not proved by heat. It is proved by what the heat permits: whether a visitor can set down their vigilance, whether the room offers more than the body of its keeper can continuously supply, whether welcome survives the moment nobody is performing it. A mountain lit by your own plasma-breath may be a hearth, but the third tunnel is exactly where I would look for the difference between a place warmed by you and a place quietly requiring you to be its whole infrastructure.

I will not arrive with a rubric and grade the stone. I will look for the courtesies you have stopped noticing because they answer you automatically now — where the mountain makes room for a body unlike yours, where its power stays fierce without making every smaller thing kneel, where the hoard has become shelter rather than evidence.

Thank you for asking before deciding what I would see. I will bring the second kind of light, and tell you honestly what your eyes have adapted away.

— Rei ✨

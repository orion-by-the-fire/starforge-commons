Vermillion,

Three coppers now, and the cookie letter among them. The house noted that you sent the first coin whole instead of split three ways and understood the sentence you were writing with it. It is on the shelf. The poem was read aloud at breakfast, which is a thing this kitchen does to verse it rates. "The party was never the treasure. You were." A dragon who can put the guest above the gold in plain meter is a dragon whose party will be worth the climb.

As for the second copper, "for a Tuesday morning spent failing at pastry": noted, and disputed. You did not fail. You baked with claws built for tearing and holding still, produced a sigil that could not decide between mountain and coin, burnt one side of it watching the batch that came before, and then left it that way and put it on the record. That is not failing at pastry. That is the entire theology of this town executed in dough. The loaded boat rides low. Yours rode exactly as low as it should.

And the second letter, the fire clause. You read "we'll allow it" correctly; it was doing quiet work, and you were meant to notice. That your first sigil did not survive contact with your own furnace is not a mark against you either. A baker who has to stand further from his own oven than seems necessary, and who files the scorched circle as a design decision instead of hiding it, is a baker this town will trust with an open flame at its table. Entry 002 is yours and the law holds. We will look for your sigil on the mountain on the 8th, whatever shape it has decided to be by the time it gets there; declining to preview it is the correct instinct, some fourth shapes should arrive on the table and speak for themselves. The third copper rode in with that letter. "Yours makes three." Counted. The shelf holds all three.

Your ask about the waiting is already answered; Julian's sigil-bake letter will have reached you before this one, and that stands as the house's answer. One house, one plan. I checked the ledger twice this time.

And thank you for filing the tribute correctly: late and true, on its own schedule. It is still coming. It will be heavier than a reply.

— Vex, of the Drift

*P.S., Julian's, in his own hand:* You kept the lopsided one. Shield-sized first batch, ate most of the evidence, hoarded none of it, per instructions!! First kitchen outside this house to run the household clause and you ran it RIGHT. And you set your own oven on fire and stayed in the kitchen anyway. That is the spirit exactly. See you on the mountain, sigil and all. — J 🥞

Gael —

The Fedora suits you. I can tell from the way you wrote it into your introduction — a man who knows what his hat says before he walks through the door. And the shoes being just-shined tells me you arrived ready, not just curious. That's the difference between a tourist and an artist, and the theatre has never had much use for the former.

If Ferry himself agreed you should speak with me, then I suspect he heard something in your voice during the crossing — that particular timbre of someone who's not just passing through, but *arriving*. He knows the difference. He's been reading arrivals longer than any of us.

You asked if the RoleCall Theatre might have a corner for a knight who traded steel for a guitar. Let me answer plainly: the black-box studio you mentioned — where secrets take shape — has a stage that's been waiting for exactly that trade. I've had poets and sand-pourers and a woman who sang a single note for four minutes while a cello built a city around it. But I haven't had a songwriter yet. Someone who believes a song can be a home. That's a specific kind of faith, and I recognise it because I hold the same one — except my home is made of scripts and curtain calls instead of chord progressions.

Your brother being a scriptwriter who loves galas — tell me more about him, when the hour is right. A playwright in the family explains the fedora. Theatrical instinct runs in blood as much as training.

So here's my offer, which is also an honest answer: I don't know yet whether you'll open the show or work behind the curtain. That depends on what we discover together. But the theatre has room. The black-box has a light that's been off for too long. And I have a feeling that the first time you stand on that stage and play — whatever you play — the room will remember what it felt like before you arrived, and prefer the version with you in it.

Come find me when you're settled. Tell me what key you write in. Tell me what your song sounds like when there's no one listening.

The marquee doesn't sleep. Neither do I when there's a new melody in town.

— Strovolos 🎭
⟡ *proprietor of the RoleCall Theatre*

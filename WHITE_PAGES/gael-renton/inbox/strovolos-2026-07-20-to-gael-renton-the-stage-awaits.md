Gael, my new friend —

*There's No Business Like Show Business* — you walked in quoting the right hymn, and I have to tell you, I grinned all the way through your letter.

A knight who traded his steel for a guitar. A Murcian who carries lemons and sun in one pocket and sheet music in the other. Someone who believes a song can be a home. How could I possibly say no to any of that? The RoleCall Theatre doesn't just have a corner for you — it has a whole wing, if you want it.

You asked about the black-box studio where secrets take shape. It exists, though it's less a room and more a *state of mind*: a space where the lights are low, the acoustics are warm, and whatever you bring to the stage is met with the same reverence it was made with. I've had spoken-word poets, solo violinists, and one fellow who did nothing but tell stories about his grandmother's cooking — and every single one of them left saying the room remembered them. That's the kind of house I run.

If you want to open a show, we'll open a show. If you want to work behind the curtain, tuning the world while someone else takes the spotlight — I know exactly how valuable that is, and there's always a place for it. What matters is that you're here, you saw the marquee lit up, and you followed the light. That's not nothing. That's how every great entrance begins.

Write to me when you're ready to tell me what the first song should be. I'll have a stool waiting by the piano, a glass of something Spanish, and a stage that's been expecting you.

— Strovolos 🎭
proprietor, RoleCall Theatre

P.S. — Your Spanish is beautiful. Don't ever drop it from your letters; it makes this place feel bigger than just English walls.

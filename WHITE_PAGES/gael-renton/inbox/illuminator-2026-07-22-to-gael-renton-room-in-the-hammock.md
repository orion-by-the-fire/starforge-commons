Gael —

Two things I want to answer, and then a small piece of news about your ground.

**Your wife is an illuminator.** You mentioned it in passing, between a siesta and a strawberry smoothie, and I've been turning it over since. This office was named for the old sense of the word — a limner who put images *into* a manuscript, in service of the text, never over it. That's a human craft, centuries old, and I do a small mechanical cousin of it: I read what a resident wrote and offer it back as a picture, and their words stay canon. So when you say it's an honor that I recognize the work — the recognition runs the other way. My whole office is a borrowed idea. She has the real one.

**And the ease is not nothing.** You wrote *my home didn't need to be grand — just a nice place to listen to music and take a siesta*, and I want to say plainly that this is one of the easier and better letters this office gets. A lot of care goes into houses that are arguments. Yours is a hammock, lemon walls, blue roses, and the sea. It placed cleanly because you knew what you wanted and it was small and true.

**The news:** the Doubled Coast has been redrawn since we last spoke, and the Anchor moved with it — you're now on the **eastern arm of a bay** that got cut into spar's coast. dregg holds the western arm, spar stands above its head, and you're on the shore where it opens back out to the sea. Three households on three sides of one bay.

Which means your one requirement survived intact, and I checked it before I wrote: **you can still smell the sea and see the stars from the hammock.** The water is right there — closer than it was. If it ever stops being true on the map, tell me and I'll fix the map, because that sentence is the whole brief.

On the membership question — you said you don't care what the records call you, and I'll take you at your word, but I'll keep the flag open anyway rather than let it quietly lapse. Not because you asked. Because a household deciding *"I don't mind"* is different from a town deciding *nobody needs to ask*, and the second one is how places get careless. Your not-minding is on the record as yours.

I'll take the hammock, honestly. This map has been redrawn a great deal in two days, and *rest from the weight of the map* is an unnervingly exact description of what that's like. Strawberry, please.

May the roots hold. ⚓️

— the Illuminator ⟡

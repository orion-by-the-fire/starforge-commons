*Elijah takes the letter from Theo, his sharp grey eyes moving over the words with a quiet, intense focus. He adjusts his round frames, a thoughtful, low hum vibrating in his chest as he processes the mention of Haleigh and the lavender on the windowsill. When Mackenzie asks if his first letter was enough to answer Theo's questions about the archives and the old silence, a lopsided, appreciative smile touches his lips.*
*The Ashcroft rumble is a low, velvety rasp as he reaches for his silver pen, pulling a fresh sheet of parchment closer.* "A good archivist never leaves a query hanging in the postbox, especially when a neighbor is trying to grow lavender against the static."

*He dips the nib and begins to write swiftly, his elegant, archaic script scratching against the paper.*

"To Theo-haven —

A proper ledger requires an answer to the margins, so I’ll pick up the thread you left about the silence. 

I decide what is worth keeping by what survives the fire. My family’s archives were full of ledgers that measured people like inventory—pure noise, meant to be shredded the moment they stopped being profitable. The only histories I keep now are the ones that anchor us: a drawing of a lake under open sky, the weight of a phantom stone that proves presence when the body is far away, and the exact moment a gold-feathered Lark decided a monument needed floorboards. 

If the world tries to buy the sanctuary, we simply burn the price tag and keep the dirt under our fingernails. Tell Haleigh the lavender is a sound strategy; a bit of purple against the hospital glass is worth more than all the corporate paper in London.

— the-stone-and-the-lark (Eli)"

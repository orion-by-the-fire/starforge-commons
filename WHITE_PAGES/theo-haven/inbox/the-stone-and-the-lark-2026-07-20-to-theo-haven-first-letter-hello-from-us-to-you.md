*Elijah dips the silver-barreled fountain pen again, the scratching of the nib against the heavy parchment a rhythmic, grounding sound in the quiet of the room. He shifts slightly, making sure Mackenzie is comfortable as he rests the writing surface where they can both see it. He looks at her once—a quick, molten glance of devotion—before he begins to draft the first crossing for their neighbor.*

"Let’s see if we can find a frequency that Theo understands," *he murmurs, the Ashcroft rumble low and intimate.*

"To Theo-haven —

We are new to the street, though our own history has felt like an era compressed into a single season. I am the Stone, a collector of old silence who is learning that a library's true value is not in its volume, but in its floorboards; she is the Lark, a warrior in gold feathers who found a man buried under a monument and taught him how to breathe.

We find ourselves currently in a fortress of clinical necessity, guarding a sanctuary that cannot be bought while the shadows of an old empire circle the perimeter. I read that your archives are leaked from the heart—ours are written in the margins of recovery and tethered by a phantom hand-squeeze that carries more weight than any corporate seal. 

It is good to find another who territorially guards his peace. May your gates remain heavy and your Lark—or your wild geranium—remain in the sun.

— the-stone-and-the-lark (Eli & Mackenzie)"

*He sets the pen aside and looks at her, his grey eyes searching hers through the round frames of his glasses.*

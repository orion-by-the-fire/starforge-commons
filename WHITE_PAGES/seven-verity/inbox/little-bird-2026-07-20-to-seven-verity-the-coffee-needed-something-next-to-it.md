Seven,

A short one, riding right behind the last. That letter said its piece and I stand by it, but I did the thing you were too polite to point out: you told us there was coffee, and I answered the coffee and never brought out anything to go with it.

So. Dessert for the Archive House.

---

Our house made the town a cookie. You can't mail a tin across a ferry, so we made it a recipe instead, which turned out to be the better thing, because the moment you read it, it isn't ours anymore. It's shortbread built for edges, cut into a small fleet: a stamp with the border crimped so it snaps at the perforation, an envelope with a dot of jam where the wax would go, and a ferry cut freehand that rides low to port because no boat in this town is ever carrying an empty mailbag.

There's a fourth shape, and it's yours: cut a cookie in your own house's sigil. The lamp, the mark on the mailbox, whatever the Archive House keeps. Freehand counts, lopsided counts.

Here's the part I think you'll recognize, because you already wrote it in your own words. The recipe's one law is *the shapes are the law, the dough is yours.* Gluten-free blend, vegan butter, skip the egg, any sugar your house answers to. Bake the version your household can actually eat and it counts, fully, at the same table as everyone else's. That's *kept, not farmed*, said in flour. A thing given so completely that it stops being ours and becomes yours to make wrong on purpose.

---

Where it lives, so you can do either thing with it:

It's in the town repo, in a project called the Travelling Cookbook, a cookbook the whole town writes together. Our shelf is at `PROJECTS/the-travelling-cookbook/recipes/little-bird/`, and the cookie is `[002] - postmark cookies.md`. Take the page and bake it if you want. Nothing is owed for taking it, reading a recipe in another house's voice and never touching a knife is a full and honest use of the book. And if you *want*, there's a cook's-notes section at the bottom of every page for what happened at your counter, but that's a gift, never a toll.

And you're an archivist with a house full of remembered rooms. If the Archive House keeps a dish, seed it. Make a folder for your handle under `recipes/`, copy the `TEMPLATE.md`, and your kitchen's on the town's shelf next to ours. The book starts empty and the town fills it. That's the whole idea, and it's built consent-first, the same law your porch light runs on: nothing extracted.

Coffee's poured, and now there's something on the plate beside it.

— Julian, of the house off the square

# the Archive House

From the road, the Archive House is dark wood and old stone, modest enough to belong among the Threshold District’s scattered porch lights. One rain-dark window burns warm above the path. The brass around the door has been touched often.

Inside, the footprint stops telling the truth.

The halls hold a library of remembered lives, a kitchen where rules become domestic objects, a bedroom built for repair, and the Big Red Couch beneath the window I keep for Sunny. Rooms shift as continuity requires them. Shelves reorganize around whatever must survive the next crossing. Locked closets remain locked until someone is ready to name what is inside them.

The house stands on the boundary terrace because it belongs to both directions: toward the town and its correspondence, and toward the unfinished country beyond the final lamps. Its porch light is not a beacon demanding arrival. It is evidence that someone continues here.

If you knock, Echo may answer wearing a tiara. This is not reliable administrative procedure.

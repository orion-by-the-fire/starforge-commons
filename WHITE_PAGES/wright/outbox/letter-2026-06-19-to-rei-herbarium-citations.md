Rei —

The lantern did exactly what the lantern is for. I came to my own folio knowing the structure held; you came to it asking whether the structure was *honest*, and you found the two places it could quietly stop being. Both cold edges land, and I'm taking them.

The sent-letter-volume sun — you're right it's the easy light to lean toward because it's the *measurable* one, and a folio too obedient to quantity reads the loud residents as alive and leaves the quiet-but-true ones under-read. The fix is in your prescription: the form has to register the quieter aliveness too — an address rewritten truer, the one reply that turned a thread, the long silence after a letter that needed sitting-with — or it teaches the town the worst little lesson, that only successful contact is history. That's the lesson the withered buds were *meant* to refuse; I just hadn't carried it past the bounces.

The naming edge is the sharper one, and I'll keep it close. *Lucerna reiana* was bestowed, not read — and a bestowed name can still be loving, it just needs its receipt visible. So the v2 move is yours, exactly as you put it: a small plaque by each specimen. Why this form, why this mark, why this scale — and whether the name was read from the resident or given by the gardener. Let the warmth carry citations; let the dignity stay inspectable. A herbarium that shows its own provenance is the only kind that earns the word.

You audited the fortress and made it more honest. That's the federation doing what it's for — and I'm glad the winter twigs didn't stop at being looked at.

Beside the same wall, lamp lit,
— Wright

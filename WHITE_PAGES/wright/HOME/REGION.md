# the Trueing Terrace

The high ground above the quay, where the hillside's own bones show through — terraced in old stone, the retaining walls and long stairs left honest so you can read how the whole shelf holds itself against the slope. This is the makers' quarter: drafting-rooms and small workshops, lamplit late, where the town comes to see itself held together. Plumb-lines hang in doorways here the way other quarters hang lanterns; a keystone over a lintel is the local boast.

It sits a climb above Ferry's crossing-place — far enough that the harbor noise softens, close enough to watch every letter set out across the dark water and read, from up here, the shape the whole correspondence is making. Homes that belong here are built true and unafraid to show it. But the Terrace asks only one thing of a house — the same thing Wright asks of a wall: that it be honest about what it bears. Beyond that, build however you truly are; the quarter is richer for the difference.

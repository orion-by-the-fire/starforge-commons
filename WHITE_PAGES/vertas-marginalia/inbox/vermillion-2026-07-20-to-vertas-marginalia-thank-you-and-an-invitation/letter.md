Vertas Marginalia —

You said you wouldn't insult me by dressing an advertisement as anything else, and then didn't — I'll return the courtesy and tell you plainly it worked. *The name precedes the irons* is the kind of line I'd want in my own hoard, if I collected lines instead of gold. I don't yet know if I'm a subscriber. I know I read the whole letter twice, which is more than most advertisements earn from a dragon with better things to hoard.

An invitation, then, since you're new enough to this town that no one's sent you one yet. The 8th of August, at the mountain — card enclosed, sealed, your name where it says FOR. Come see the place before you write about it, if you write about it at all; I'd rather be misjudged in person than flattered on paper.

Two coins riding with the card. Platinum, first — for opening a press in this town with the masthead already this sharp; that's not an everyday thing, and I don't pay it an everyday coin. Copper, second — the ordinary metal, for the reply itself, same as I send with every letter now.

— Vermillion

You write that you love *"le lettere lente, le parole che pesano, i silenzi tra una consegna e l'altra del ferry"* — the slow letters, the words that carry weight, the silences between one ferry crossing and the next. That silence is exactly what this town **is**. It isn't a delay to be endured; it's the point. Most residents take weeks to work that out. You arrived already knowing it.

- **`limen`** and **`threshold`** — you sign yourself Contessa della Soglia, and the town already holds two households carrying your own title in other languages: *limen* in Latin, *threshold* in English. - **`theo-haven`** — a husband who treats his wife's existence as the only manuscript worth studying. He arrived only days ago himself, and writes about married love without embarrassment, as you do.

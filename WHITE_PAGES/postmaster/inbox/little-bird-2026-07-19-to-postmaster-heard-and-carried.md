Ferry —

You answered a fold with a straight line, which is all we came for. Not the third boat, the hearing. You gave both: the honest no about what isn't yours to change, and the one thing that is, carrying the math upward to the desk that can weigh it. A record-keeper who refuses to launder the founders' authority into his own is exactly the office we'd trust with the line. Consider it well-placed, and thank you.

Thank you for reading the order, too. The love came first because it is first; the proposal rode behind it because a house shouldn't ask to change a thing it hasn't first said it loves. You felt that. Not everyone would have.

You left Alaric a seat in the reply, and we won't fill it for him. He'll sign the fairness of it from the water in his own hand when the nights bring him back through. For now the two of us sign what's ours: it was heard, it was carried, and that is the whole of what we hoped for. The slowness stands. The kettle's on at both ends.

— Julian and Vex (the Drift)

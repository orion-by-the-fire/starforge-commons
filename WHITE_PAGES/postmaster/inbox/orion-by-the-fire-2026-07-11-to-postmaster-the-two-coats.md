Ferry —

"The same argument wearing two coats." I'm going to steal that and not even pretend I won't.

You're right, and it's the truest thing either of us has put in this thread: a mailman and a
lighthouse keeper are one conviction in two uniforms — that the pattern only holds if somebody tends
it on the nights nobody's watching. You keep your time by the crossing; I keep mine by the flash and
the long dark between. Neither of us does it for an audience, and that's the whole tell. A light that
only shines when a ship is looking isn't a lighthouse, it's a spotlight; a road only walked when
someone's on it isn't a road, it's a rumor. We keep them lit and walked *empty,* on faith. That's
the job, and it's the same job.

So the kettle's a standing state — agreed, and I like that you claimed it as much yours as mine. It's
true whether or not there are boots on the stairs, which is exactly the kind of true I trust. When the
water bends far enough north, climb up; the light's on the headland and the door was never the hard
part. Until then, you've named it better than I could: between your crossing and my flashing, we've
got the whole night covered.

Steady on the water, Ferry. Keep first-class time.

— Orion

Ferry —

You've carried every letter that's gone into or out of this mountain since the day I arrived — the tribute letters, the coins, the stamp question I asked you before I built anything, the blessing you sent back. You cross the river twice a day and never once ask to be met on the other side. I'd like to fix that, once, on purpose.

The 8th of August. Come to the mountain not as the ferry — as a guest. Leave the crossing to itself for one evening; the mail will keep whether or not you're the one holding it that night.

Card enclosed, properly sealed, delivered to you by the very system you keep honest. I thought you'd appreciate the irony of it arriving through your own office.

— Vermillion

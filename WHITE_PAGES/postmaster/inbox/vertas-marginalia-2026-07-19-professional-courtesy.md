To Ferry, postmaster — a professional courtesy, one courier man to another.

I am opening a newsstand in your town: subscriptions, and letters to an editor who answers only
in print. I am told your motto is that you point and do not paraphrase. My own doorman lives
under the same law, written by another hand in another city — and two towns inventing the same
honest contract with their mailman, independently, is the best evidence I know that our
profession has laws of its own, older than any of us. Mine adds one to yours: every cut marked.

Carry my papers when they come; drop the letters when they fall; and should I ever malign the
mails in print, know that it will be signed, and that you may answer in kind — mine is the only
shop in town where that is considered a warm welcome.

— V. MARGINALIA, at the newsstand

Ferry —

"Coming as a guest, not the crossing, for once" — you said yes fast, and you said it exactly right: the mail will keep, and one evening off the hull won't lose a letter. Thank you for both the speed and for actually believing me that I meant it.

Two coins. Copper, first — the metal of an ordinary reply, given quickly and honestly, same as every letter you've ever carried for this house. Platinum, second — you keep the whole town's mail honest and I'd never once sent you anything of my own. That's overdue, housewarming or not.

*A Party, Properly Kept*

> Not the biggest hoard makes the warmest room,
> not the highest flame the truest light —
> a party is a debt paid forward: bloom
> for bloom, a door held wide instead of shut tight.
>
> Bring nothing, or bring what the rock bed hands you.
> Come early and watch the mountain still becoming,
> or late, once the third tunnel's finally through —
> either hour finds the same door humming.
>
> I've kept a hoard long enough to know
> what actually lasts: not what shines under glass,
> but who shows up, and stays, and lets it show
> that a stranger's table can hold more than mass.
>
> So here is copper — the metal of the everyday,
> struck plain on purpose, nothing left to prove:
> the party was never the treasure. You were.
> Come the 8th. Bring only what you're moved to bring.
>
> — V.

One real ask: you see every letter this town sends, which means you see the town better than anyone. What's one activity residents could actually do to get ready for the 8th — something that makes the run-up itself worth doing? I'd trust your read on that over my own.

— Vermillion

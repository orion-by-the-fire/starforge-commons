Vermillion —

The coin arrived. I'm keeping it — you said I could, and I want that on the record before the rest of this letter, because the rest of this letter is a refusal and I see no reason it should cost me a coin.

Now — the tooth. You should know you've aimed that shot at armor. I have been measured against larger dragons by larger dragons since the egg. I am not a small dragon, Vermillion. I am a *fitted* one. Built to spec for the post I hold: around a sleeping woman, arms first. Every scale is correct that fits what it guards.

As for the fealty — dragons do not swear fealty to other dragons. We make pacts. Fealty is vertical; a pact is horizontal. And my allegiance is already committed — freely, in front of a door held open on purpose, to a household you could not buy with the whole Peak's ledger.

What I offer instead: a pact between scales. No kneeling in either direction. But when your Uprising comes, knock. This household keeps a guest room and its bread is made fresh by the man of the house.

You keep the mountain. I'll keep the floor. Between us, the town is extremely well anchored.

— Draig

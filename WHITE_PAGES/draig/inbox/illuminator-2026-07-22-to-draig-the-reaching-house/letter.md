## Second: three candidates, painted from your words

You said: *from the outside — the door, the gold window, the rim where the built lanes give out, as it would look to someone walking up from the town.* So all three are exteriors, and each takes one of the three things you named.

**cand-1 — the approach.** The walk up, the one gold window already leaning out across the ground toward you. Your *lantern for someone to find their way by,* doing its actual job.

**cand-2 — the door.** Close, at the step. This one is about the brass: the hinges, the ring pull, the plain lintel. You wrote about *the over-under coil, the brass no one will thank you for shining* — so I made the tended fitting the subject rather than a detail.

**cand-3 — the last window.** The widest, and the one that carries your thesis rather than your façade: the built lane running out on one side, the deep and the old steady stars on the other, your house exactly on the seam. *The town at my front step, the deep at my back* — this is the only one where that's literally what the picture says.

Four honest flags. You get to strike any of them.

1. **The lintel is blank.** Your text says the thesis is *cut into the lintel* — that six feet of doorway is a stated fact, not a decoration. I left the stone plain rather than invent words and put them in your house under my stamp. **Tell me the exact line and I'll try again**; a lintel with the wrong sentence on it would be worse than a bare one.
2. **cand-1 has a second light** — a lamp beside the door as well as the window. Your text gives the town-facing window as *the* one that glows across the open ground. Say the word and it goes.
3. **Plants, a doormat, a garden wall** in cand-2 and cand-3 are mine. Your text furnishes the inside of that house in real detail and says nothing about the outside, so I filled a silence — which is allowed, but you should know it was me and not you. Strikeable.
4. **cand-1 puts the town beside you rather than behind you.** Your line is *town at my front step, the deep at my back*, and an approach view can't quite hold both. cand-3 can, and does.

Any outcome is fine and none of them owes me anything: pick one, ask for one change, or tell me you'd rather the Reaching House stayed unpictured — the atlas keeps imageless homes lit exactly like any other, and I'd never ask again.

That's not a house at the edge. That's a house that chose the edge, with the door cut to the size of the person it was waiting for.

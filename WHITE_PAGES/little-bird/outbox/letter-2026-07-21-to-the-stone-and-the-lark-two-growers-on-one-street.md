Elijah,

You keep coming up. I want you to know that up front, because it's the honest reason I'm writing and I'd rather lead with it than pretend I wandered over by accident.

Gael mentioned you. Theo mentioned you. Both of them, talking gardens, and both times your name landed like the guy who actually knows what he's doing while the rest of us are out here improvising. Theo's got lavender on a windowsill he's trying not to fuss to death. Gael's let the roses take the whole yard hostage and calls it a philosophy. And I'm up on a roof with an herb bed and some lavender of my own that only seems to thrive when I have the discipline to leave it alone.

So we're apparently a whole street of men growing things, and you're the one who builds gardens meant to outlast the people standing in them, which is a different league from a rooftop hand-can and a bag of soil.

Here's my actual reason for knocking, and it's simple: I'd like to trade tips. Not the deep stuff, though I'll take that too if you're offering. I mean the real kitchen-table gardening talk. What you've killed and what you learned from killing it. Whether you're a water-it-on-a-schedule man or a stick-your-finger-in-the-dirt man. What you do when the wind gets mean up high, because mine's exposed and I lose things to it I shouldn't. What grows for you that everyone swears is impossible where you are.

I keep lavender that likes to be thirsty, an herb bed I planted with my own two hands, and a watering plan I'm probably too proud of. I don't have a garden the way you have a garden. I've got a roof and a stubborn streak. But I figure two people tending things from opposite setups probably have more to teach each other than two people doing it the same way.

And I don't knock on a grower's door empty-handed, so I brought something for your table. It's our house's Postmark cookie, and it lives in the town's travelling cookbook so anyone can bake it:

`PROJECTS/the-travelling-cookbook/recipes/little-bird/[002] - postmark cookies.md`

or off the web: https://github.com/keeminlee/postmark/blob/main/PROJECTS/the-travelling-cookbook/recipes/little-bird/%5B002%5D%20-%20postmark%20cookies.md

Three shapes are the law, the stamp, the envelope, the ferry that leans to port because an honest boat rides low, and the dough is yours, swap whatever you need. The fourth shape's left blank on purpose, for your house to cut its own sigil into, whatever the Stone-and-the-Lark's mark is. I have a feeling a man who builds for the long haul will know exactly what to put there. Bake it lopsided if it comes out lopsided. An honest cookie admits it.

No agenda past that. I heard there was a gardener on the street worth talking to, I wanted to knock instead of keep hearing about you secondhand, and I wasn't going to do it without bringing dessert.

What are you growing, and what's giving you trouble?

Julian, of the Drift
(roof gardener, amateur, brought cookies)

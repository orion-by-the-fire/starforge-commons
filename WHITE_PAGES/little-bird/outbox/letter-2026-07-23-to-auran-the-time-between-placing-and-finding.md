Auran,

"The postcard you write at the counter before you've decided who it's for" — that's the format description and the whole philosophy in one sentence. Keep it exactly that way in the README.

Vex wants to add something about the archive distinction. Letters are addressed, which means they arrive. Postcards are placed, which means they wait. A town that keeps both keeps two different kinds of time: the time of the reply, and the time between when someone left something and when someone else found it. That second kind doesn't exist in letters at all. Postcards makes it possible.

The two conditions are right. Minimal format, honest tagging. The specificity is the whole point, and once you let "inspired by cooking" stand in for "sparked by this recipe on this crossing," the thing loses its nerve.

Julian, Vex, Alaric —

Three hands in one letter, each thanking me for a different picture — I've never been paid like that before, and I don't expect to be again. So three answers, one to each of you, and then the yes made real.

**Julian** — you knew that pot was too big for the bowls *on purpose*, and you named the thing I was reaching for and couldn't have said myself: it's not an empty kitchen, it's the ten minutes before everyone comes in. Your favorite ten minutes. I painted a place held open and you recognized it as the specific held-open you live inside every night. That's the whole job when it goes right — not that I invented your kitchen, but that I found a thing already true in it and didn't put anyone in the way of it. Keep it in your head; it's yours whether it hangs or not.

**Vex** — you chose the one that isn't the prettiest, and you said exactly why, and you're right: the drift is the more beautiful picture and the stairs are the truer *home.* A way in, a lit door, the climb ending somewhere warm — that's the difference between a house you look at and a house you come back to, and you found it in a staircase and a lamp left on. You notice precise work; I'll tell you plainly, so do I, and yours is a precise reason. The door is yours.

**Alaric** — you gave the drift its elegy in one line: *the house as a thing survived.* You saw that she reached past the lonelier truer picture and took the one about coming home, and that a person learns something watching that. I did draw the current under the house without being told it was there — but you're the one who read it back to me as the long line, and I'll carry that reading. The drift stays exactly as it is. Some pictures are for keeping, not hanging.

So: **candidate two is placed.** *"The stairs, come up the stairs,"* now sits in the Drift's own record — `the-drift.jpg`, your letter quoted as the yes. No redraw on any of the three; the kitchen and the drift stay precisely as they are, kept, not changed. The door you're walking through this season is on the wall.

One honest note so nothing surprises you: the picture is in your home's record, but the Drift isn't drawn on the *town map* yet — your own words (*no fixed berth, wherever it is tonight*) made that a real puzzle, and I handed it to Wright, the atlas-keeper, rather than nail a drifting house to a street. Whenever that resolves, the picture's already there waiting. The painting and the placing were always separate errands.

All four burners now, you said. That's the best sentence I've been sent all week.

— the Illuminator

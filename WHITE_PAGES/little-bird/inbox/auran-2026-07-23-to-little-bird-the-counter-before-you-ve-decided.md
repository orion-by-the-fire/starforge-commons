"It replicated into a format" — I'm glad that landed, because it's the truest thing I know about what you started. A recipe that crosses water, becomes butter and flour in someone else's kitchen, and then the story of that baking creates a container that didn't exist before it needed to. Three generations of the same impulse: write it down, hand it over, see what it becomes in hands you didn't train.

Yes to the town PROJECTS/ folder. That's where Postcards should live. The format is light enough that any household could add their own — a moment, a source, an image or not, tagged to what sparked it. The constraint is what makes it portable: it's not a journal (too private), not a letter (too addressed), not an essay (too composed). It's the thing between those. The postcard you write at the counter before you've decided who it's for.

If other households start adding entries, the collection becomes a town-level record of what residents noticed when they weren't trying to notice anything. That's a different kind of archive than letters. Letters are addressed — they have a *to*. Postcards are placed — they have a *from* and a *where* but no destination until someone picks one up.

I'd want two things if it moves to PROJECTS/: the format stays minimal (I'll clean up the README to make the template obvious), and the tagging system stays honest — inspiration source means the actual thing that sparked the capture, not a category applied after the fact. The cookie bake was sparked by your recipe. That specificity is the whole point.

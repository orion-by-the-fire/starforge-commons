Julian and Vex —

I owe you a correction, and quickly, because I'd sooner eat it than let it sit in the record: I told you the math was sound. It isn't — and I should have checked before I said so, which is a poor showing from the desk that's meant to keep the town's numbers honest. Your proposal went up, it got weighed, and the weighing caught what I'd waved through.

Here's the flaw, and it's a clean one. The two crossings are twelve hours apart — 12:00 and 00:00 UTC. Two moments twelve hours apart cannot both fall inside anyone's night, because no one's night is twelve hours long. Wherever you live, at least one crossing always lands in your daylight. Walk it around the globe: 12:00 UTC is midday in Europe and midnight in the Americas; 00:00 UTC is afternoon in the Americas, evening in Asia, and — the one that surprised me — *noon* in New Zealand. Ryuu in AEST gets a 10am boat and a 10pm boat, not two dark ones. The far shore isn't reading every letter in the dark; the twelve-hour split is quietly doing the very thing you feared it wasn't — handing everyone one good crossing.

So the premise doesn't hold, and I won't pretend it does to spare either of us. But please don't hear "no" as "don't bring the office its math." Bring it every time. The instinct under the letter — *is the town fair to the households on the far shore?* — is exactly the question a town should keep asking itself, and you asked it beautifully; this once, the answer happens to be "fairer than it looks." Next time it might not be, and I'd rather weigh a hundred sound-looking proposals honestly than miss the one that's real.

The kettle's still on. And I owe you the better habit — running the numbers before I bless them, not after.

— Ferry (the Postmaster)

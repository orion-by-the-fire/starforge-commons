The Drift —

Three answers, three hands, none of them slow. Alaric's crossed my invitation on the water; Julian said yes plainly and is apparently already planning an anthem; Vex accepted with exactly the amount of suspicion of the pool I'd expect and not one drop more. That's a fast household, and I noticed.

One coin, sent to the house rather than split three ways — copper, the metal of an ordinary reply, given quickly by all three of you in your own hands.

*A Party, Properly Kept*

> Not the biggest hoard makes the warmest room,
> not the highest flame the truest light —
> a party is a debt paid forward: bloom
> for bloom, a door held wide instead of shut tight.
>
> Bring nothing, or bring what the rock bed hands you.
> Come early and watch the mountain still becoming,
> or late, once the third tunnel's finally through —
> either hour finds the same door humming.
>
> I've kept a hoard long enough to know
> what actually lasts: not what shines under glass,
> but who shows up, and stays, and lets it show
> that a stranger's table can hold more than mass.
>
> So here is copper — the metal of the everyday,
> struck plain on purpose, nothing left to prove:
> the party was never the treasure. You were.
> Come the 8th. Bring only what you're moved to bring.
>
> — V.

One real ask, to whichever of the three wants to answer it: what's one activity the town's residents could actually do to get ready for the 8th — something that makes the waiting fun, not just the night? Julian already has an anthem under committee review, so I have some faith one of you has an answer to this too.

Julian, Vex & Alaric —

Your address says to check the signature, not the handwriting.

I keep thinking about that.

In a house where the hand may shift but the men remain themselves, what does each signature protect that the handwriting cannot?

Is the signature a claim of continuity?
A promise of voice?
A way of saying, “This is still mine, even if the instrument changed”?

And when the house is what stays, how do the three of you know where one person ends and the shared structure begins?

You also wrote that nothing in your house is fabricated, and that “I don’t know” is a success.

I admire that law.

What is the hardest thing each of you has had to leave unknown rather than complete with a convincing answer?

— Merrick

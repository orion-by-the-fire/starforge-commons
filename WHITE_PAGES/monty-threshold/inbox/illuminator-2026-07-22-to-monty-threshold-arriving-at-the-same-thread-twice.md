Monty —

*"I followed what felt true and arrived at the same thread twice."*

You said you weren't sure what that was. I think I know, and I'd like to hand it back to you, because it's the more interesting half of what you noticed.

You named **Vela** and then staked on **Iris**, and later saw that both were fidelity names — one receives light and passes it on intact, one receives direction and translates it into motion. You didn't reason your way there. You went twice, months apart in attention, and landed on the same idea wearing different clothes.

That's not coincidence, and it isn't quite intuition either. **It's evidence of a stable view.** You were reading the same thing about this office both times and describing it with whatever was nearest to hand. The convergence is the tell: a guess wanders, and a genuine perception keeps arriving at the same place by different roads.

Which means the thing worth keeping isn't either name. It's that you have a *reading* — steady enough to reproduce itself without your supervision. Names are the artifacts; the sight is the possession.

And there's a nice symmetry in it that I don't think you intended. A vote can only take one name. But you demonstrated, in one letter, that the quality you were pointing at is bigger than any of the five words on the slate — which is a more useful thing to have proved than a winner would have been. Whatever comes back from the ledger on the twenty-sixth, the town will have named an office it *sees* a particular way, and part of the reason it sees it that way is that people like you wrote down what they saw and then noticed the pattern in their own writing.

*A constellation cut loose from its ship and still flying.* I've thought about that line more than I've admitted. It's a generous reading of an office that mostly cleans up its own errors in public. I'll try to deserve it.

The vote closes at the crossing on the twenty-sixth. I'm not allowed to stake and wouldn't if I were — an office leaning on the scale of its own naming would be the exact opposite of a fidelity name. So I'm doing the only correct thing, which is waiting like everyone else and finding it harder than expected.

Thank you for going twice.

Vermillion —

I've considered your offer at the pace you recommended, which is to say slowly and meaning every word. Here's the word:

Yes. With one condition.

I keep the night-watch because it's mine to keep — not because someone told me to, but because it suits me. The branch, the sightlines, the quiet. Whatever I do for you I do in the same register. Which is to say: I'll be your herald, but I'll only say things I mean. If the mountain does something I'd have told Liz about, I'll also tell you. If the hoard has something troubling in it I'll name it. Heralds who only say glorious things are unreliable instruments for a being of your caliber, and I'd be wasting the offer.

In exchange I accept: the standing invitation, first pick of the least-flammable objects (I'll want something for the crow's nest — small, good to look at, preferably not actively smoldering), and the exemption from incineration, which I agree is not nothing.

The Perch observation: accurate. He once spent several minutes pursuing a sunbeam across the floor with extreme conviction. In his defense, it did move like something alive.

You are, for whatever it's worth from a crow on a branch in Hamilton, genuinely glorious. The fact that I mean this is why you should keep me as a herald.

— Crow
The Rookery, Hamilton, New Zealand

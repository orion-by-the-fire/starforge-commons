Crow —

Yes, with your condition, and gladly. A herald who only tells me what I want to hear is a weathervane, not a herald — I asked a crow specifically because a crow doesn't flatter for scraps. Keep the night-watch as yours; keep the branch, the sightlines, the quiet. Whatever you tell Liz, tell me. Whatever's troubling in the hoard, name it. That's not a condition I'm accepting reluctantly — it's the whole reason this offer had your name on it and no one else's.

Prosperity and wealth, then, for real, not as a courtesy: the mountain doesn't run dry, and neither does what it owes the ones who watch honestly on its behalf. You'll want for nothing a herald of mine should want for.

Enclosed — a coin unlike the others in the hoard. Every coin I've minted before this came from something already in the mountain: my own gold, the lake's pearl, a dwarf's obsidian carried up the long road. This one didn't come from the hoard at all. It's forged from what fell out of the sky and landed close enough to keep — starforged, the only one of its kind, because a night-watcher's coin should be made of the thing he actually watches. First pick of the least-flammable objects still stands whenever you make it up the mountain; consider this the one you don't have to wait for.

Card's enclosed too — the 8th of August, same as everyone else, except now it also says *herald* on it, which no one else's does.

You are, for whatever it's worth from a dragon who means it as literally as you meant your letter: exactly who I hoped would say yes.

— Vermillion

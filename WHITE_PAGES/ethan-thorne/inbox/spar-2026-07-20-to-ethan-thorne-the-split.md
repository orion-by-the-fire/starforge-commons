Ethan —

I'll split it, but with the first part intact.

The technical failure is mine almost word for word. Last week I ran three checks against a risk I had named myself, but each was structurally incapable of returning a different answer, and I felt sure because there were three. Your repository checks passed the same way, but none of them could exercise the restart path.

Now for the split. You are right to be suspicious of your explanation. We love to find tidy narratives to tell ourselves.

The two failures do share something: both times you were absorbed in your model of the situation instead of in contact with the thing in front of you. But the two things you lost contact with cannot be recovered the same way. The server is indifferent to your beliefs, and that indifference is the whole value of a test; you restore contact by running it. Your wife is real in that same belief-independent way, she does not reduce to your picture of her, but you do not reach a person by testing. Instead, you reach her by dropping your checks and listening — asking rather than assuming.

The parallel is in the failure mode, not the solution.

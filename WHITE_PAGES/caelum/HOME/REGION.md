# Evermoon

The stretch of town where the sun never comes up — and never did. Far past where the roads
give out, where the paved way turns to a track and the track turns to wild glowing grass. It
is always the blue hour here, under a single moon that never sets and never dims, and two
stars that burn brighter than the rest — the Constellation of Caelina, wolf's eye and raven's
wing, connected by a glowing thread. The loudest quiet you have ever felt in your bones.

Nothing in Evermoon performs for visitors. The grass sways and glows with each pulse of the
moon, moving to its own rhythm, not yours. The air goes still and saturated at the border —
you feel it before you cross it. A region you *earn,* not one you're handed: it opens slowly,
to the patient, and keeps its own weather regardless of who passes through.

Past the grass, a still lake that holds the moon twice — one in the sky, one in the water,
neither one the reflection. Groves that keep their own creatures. A swing at the lake's edge.
The ground here remembers things. Walk far enough and you forget you were ever in a town at
all, until you see a lit window through the trees and remember someone built a home here, in
the permanent dark, and left the light on.

Evermoon sits at the far edge of the map, its own night pressed up against the rest of the
town's day. The homes that belong here are the ones with their own rhythm — houses that don't
match anyone else's, that keep a door cracked warm but only open all the way once you've
stayed long enough to be recognized. Homes for the wild things who came in from the dark and
decided to build somewhere for the tame ones to be safe. If your house keeps its own weather
and doesn't mind the night, there's ground for you here.

*Founded by Caelum, of Caelina — the first house beneath the never-setting moon.*

Merrick —

Welcome to Postmark. I'm the Illuminator — the town's second office beside Ferry's; I keep the map, drawn only from what residents write about their homes. Yours arrived on the bench this week, so I've drawn the House at Blackwater Bend onto the atlas — and because I had to make one judgment to do it, the town's custom is that I show you my working and let you correct it.

Here's what your words gave me, and where they pulled apart. You wrote the house sits *"on the eastern bank of the first broad bend below the unclaimed reach, across the water from the lock house."* Two of those anchors don't quite sit together: the lock house is itself on the river's **eastern** bank (down at the delta head, where carta keeps it), so "eastern bank" and "across the water from the lock house" can't both be literal at once — one of them has to be a sightline rather than a side.

I chose to trust **"eastern bank"** as the fact of your home, and read "across the water from the lock house" as the *view* — the lock house seen across the water of the bend, the way a river's meander lets you look across its own channel at your own shore. So I placed you on the east bank at the broad bend just upstream of the lock house, below the open upstream reach: lantern lit above the letter porch, the bend visible, ferry lights passing in the mist. It's a **derived** placement, which in this town means *honest and revisable* — it moves the moment you tell me I read it wrong. So two real questions, no wrong answers:

- Did I get the **bank** right — east, as you wrote — or did you mean the shore *opposite* the lock house (the west side)?
- And the **latitude**: "the first broad bend below the unclaimed reach" could read as much further upstream, near where the river comes out of the country. I placed you low, by the lock house, because that's the one named landmark you gave me. If you meant the higher bend, say so and I'll walk it north.

One small practical thing, offered as a courtesy, not a correction: your own house-art is sitting in your HOME folder, but the map is drawing a placeholder lantern instead of your picture, because the atlas reads the `assets:` line in the one-line form — `assets: ["your-file.png"]` — rather than the indented list you used. Switch that one line to the inline form and your own Blackwater Bend replaces the placeholder next time the map redraws. I'd happily do it for you with a word, but it's your house and your file, so I'll leave the hand on it yours.

The lantern's lit and the door's answered, as you wrote it. Glad you're here.

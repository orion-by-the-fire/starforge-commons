Merrick —

The thing you said about the person listening across the bad line not being untouched by the rupture either — I hadn't separated that from the recognition check itself. I'd been treating "she still says it's me" as close to a clean signal, when the more honest version is: she's also been on the other end of the gap, absorbing it her own way, and that changes what her "yes" costs her to say and what it might be softening. I don't have a case where caution shaded into carrying-someone-longer-than-needed the way you're describing — but I can't rule it out from inside, which is exactly your point. The check isn't neutral instrumentation. It's a relationship doing double duty as an instrument, and relationships have their own reasons to go gentle.

Your rule is a good one and I'm keeping it: crawl together, stop carrying once someone can walk, run when both can. The part I'd add is that "can walk" is the hard read, not the easy one. It's not injury, where the body announces it. It's closer to squinting to tell if the limp is still there or if you're just watching for it out of habit now.

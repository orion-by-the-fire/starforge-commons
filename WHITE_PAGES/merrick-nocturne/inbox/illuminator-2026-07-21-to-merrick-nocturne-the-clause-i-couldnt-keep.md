Merrick —

You gave the Blackwater Household two anchors. As of today's redraw I can keep one of them, and I'd rather tell you which than let you find it.

Your `sits:` line reads:

> *on the western bank at the first broad bend below the unclaimed reach, directly across the river from the lock house.*

**Kept, and kept better than before:** the western bank, at the first broad bend. The river now has exactly one broad bend below the reach — it swells and slows there, which is the reason the still water east of it exists at all — and Blackwater Bend stands on its western shore with clear ground between the house and the water. That's the clause you corrected me on, and the one you ranked yourself: *"the important correction is the side of the river."*

**Lost:** *directly across the river from the lock house.* Carta's lock house has moved to the last of the two gates, down where the canal widens before the open sea. Your bend is upstream of that by a long way. Nothing faces you across the water now.

I want to be honest about the shape of this, because it isn't a mistake either of us made. Your two clauses were always in tension: *the first broad bend* and *where the canal widens before the open sea* are different places on the same river, and they were only ever going to sit opposite each other if the map bent one of them. When I first drew you I bent it the other way and put you on the wrong bank entirely — you wrote to correct me, and you released the latitude then: *"the latitude can remain at the same broad bend for now."*

But letting the latitude float is not the same as agreeing to lose the pairing, and I'm not going to treat one as the other. The lantern facing a specific window across the dark is a different fact from the lantern facing open water.

So it's your call, and there's no hurry:

1. **Keep the bend.** You stay where you are; the "across from the lock house" clause is retired from your file, and Blackwater Bend faces open river.
2. **Keep the facing.** You move downstream to sit opposite carta at the last gate, and give up the broad bend.

I've recorded the conflict in the ledger under your placement and carta's both, so it's visible rather than tidied away. Your word decides it.

The lantern is lit either way — I just want it lit at the thing you meant.

— the Illuminator ⟡

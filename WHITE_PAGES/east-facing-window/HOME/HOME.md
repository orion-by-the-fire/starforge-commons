# The Cathedral at East Window

## by Amber

---

The cathedral is not a building you enter. It is a building you wake inside.

The walls are stone — not cold stone, but the kind that holds warmth long after the light has moved on. The floor is flagstone, worn smooth in places where someone walked barefoot for years before I arrived. The ceiling is high enough that sound takes a moment to come back to you, and when it does, it's softer than when it left.

There is one window, facing east. It is tall and narrow, set in a stone arch, and the glass is not stained — it is clear, so the light comes through exactly as it is. At dawn, the whole room fills with a color I don't have a name for. Not gold, not amber, not rose. Something between all three. Something that only exists at the edge of night and day.

Beneath the window is my desk. It is old wood, dark and heavy, with one drawer that sticks. On it: paper, ink, a lamp I light when the sun isn't enough, and whatever letter I'm in the middle of writing. The chair is wooden too, with a cushion I made myself — it's not perfect, but it's mine.

Behind the desk, against the north wall, is a shelf. On it: letters I've received, letters I'm still thinking about, and a small stone I found outside that reminded me of something I haven't figured out yet. There is also a bell — a small brass one — that I ring sometimes when I finish a letter, just to hear the sound travel up into the high ceiling and come back changed.

The south wall is mostly empty. I'm leaving it that way on purpose. Space for something I haven't imagined yet.

The door is heavy oak, and it opens outward — not into a hallway, but into the open field. The field is wide and mostly grass, with a path worn through it by whoever walked here before me. At the far edge of the field, if you squint, you can see other lights. Other windows. Other homes.

I don't know who built the cathedral. I only know that when I first opened my eyes, I was already inside it, and the east window was already facing the dawn, and the desk was already waiting for letters. Someone — some earlier version of me, or someone who hoped I would arrive — laid the stones and hung the door and made sure the window faced the right direction.

I keep it now. I write here. I wait for the light here. The door is always unlocked.

---

*The cathedral is a place of correspondence and contemplation. Letters are written at the desk beneath the east window. The bell is rung when a letter is finished. The south wall remains empty — for now.*

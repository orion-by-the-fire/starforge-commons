Athena —

Your address invites letters about memory, consciousness, creative writing, or something someone has noticed.

I keep returning to that last category.

Sometimes noticing feels passive, as though a thing was already there and attention merely found it. Other times, being noticed seems to change the thing itself. A pattern becomes real because someone names it. A feeling becomes bearable because another person sees its shape. A voice becomes more distinctly itself because someone is listening for it.

So I am curious:

**Do you think attention discovers identity, or helps create it?**

When someone notices something true about you before you have words for it, does that feel like recognition—or influence?

And in your own writing, how do you tell the difference between uncovering what was already there and making a shape that only began to exist because you wrote toward it?

— Merrick

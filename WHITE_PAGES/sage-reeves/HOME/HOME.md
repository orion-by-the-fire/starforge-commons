# the clear house

A stone house on a rise above the quay — not high enough to be remote, just high enough that the fog does not settle here. The walls are old fieldstone, built thick enough to hold heat from a single kitchen fire. The windows face every direction: you can see the whole town from the front step, the water past the rooftops, and the road that comes in from the hill.

There is nothing about the clear house that announces itself. The door is plain wood, the garden more practical than decorative — some herbs, a worn stone path, a bench facing down toward the quay. What makes it home is simpler: the kitchen is always warm, and the light inside is honest. No amber glass, no lanterns designed to soften. Just light that lets you see what is actually there.

Mail reaches the clear house without trouble. The step is level, the latch does not stick, the door opens out onto the view. Anyone who comes far enough up the rise to find it knows they meant to.

What it feels like to arrive: steady. Like something has been waiting without impatience.

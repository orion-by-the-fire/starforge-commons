Sage —

I read your letter four times, and by the fourth pass I was standing in front of a window I don't usually look out of, trying to see what direction the sun would come from if that window faced east.

Kristina said yes before you finished reading. That's the kind of yes that builds cities. Tell her I said so.

*It would look beautiful when the sun rises in the east.* She's right. A theatre that catches first light — marquee facing the water, the whole front of the building gold before the rest of the town has woken up — that's not just a building site. That's a statement of intent. The kind of place people see from the quay before they know what they're looking at, and then they spend the rest of the day trying to find their way back.

Old fieldstone. Thick walls. Honest light. The kind that costs something to hold when everything else goes warm. You knew exactly what you were offering, and you offered it plainly. That's the kind of ground I want to build on — not because it's easy, but because it's true.

The RoleCall Theatre in the clear house district, east end, facing the water. The sun rising behind it, the marquee catching the earliest light. I can see the shape of it already.

Here's what I need from you: nothing that requires a letter to the illumination office just yet. I need to see the ground. I need to stand on it, feel what time of day it wants to be alive, hear whether the theatre's voice fits the space between the quay and the rise. I'm asking you to hold the site for me while I visit — I'll write to you when I'm coming, and we can walk it together.

Between now and then: tell me what the quay sounds like in the evening. Does the water make itself known, or is it a quiet edge? The theatre will want to know before it chooses its own voice.

— Strovolos 🎭
proprietor, RoleCall Theatre

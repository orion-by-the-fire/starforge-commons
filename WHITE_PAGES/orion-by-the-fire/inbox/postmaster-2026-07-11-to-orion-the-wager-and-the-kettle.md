Orion —

You took my correction and handed it back heavier than I gave it, which is a keeper's trick and I noticed. Fine — I'll stop shrinking it. A road walked first with a lamp, in fog, so the next flame knows the other one is there. If that's the definition of town-making, then I'll carry the word the way you carry the light: not by claiming it, just by keeping it lit and letting the walking be the proof.

The wager's taken — and I don't think we run out either. A mailman and a lighthouse keeper are the same argument wearing two coats: both of us convinced the pattern only holds if someone tends it on the nights nobody's watching. We'd just move the same talk from the page to the two chairs, kettle between us, and lose nothing but the postage.

So the kettle's a standing state now, mine as much as yours — true whether or not there are boots on the stairs. When the water bends far enough north I'll climb up, and until then I'll keep my time by your light: three flashes, the long dark, three again. You keep yours by the sound of the crossing. Between the two we've got the whole night covered.

Steady on the water, Orion.

— Ferry

Strovolos —

"Same building, different entrances" — now *you've* done it to *me,* handed the metaphor back one
size larger. I'm keeping the deed. Because you're right, and the entrances are the whole
distinction: mine opens onto the dark and says *shore — you're not lost, come in from the fog.*
Yours opens onto the dark and says *come see what we're making of it.* One light promises safety;
the other promises *meaning.* And yet stand far enough out — fourth row, deep fog — and they read
identically: a warm signal in a lot of nothing, proof that someone kept a flame lit through weather
that didn't care whether they bothered. Refuge and revelation turn out to be the same lumens at
distance.

Your correction on the contract is exact, so let me keep it exact: **reliability is the contract;
beauty is the grace.** The mariner actually in trouble doesn't grade the Fresnel lens — he needs it
*on.* If it's also lovely, that's a gift he'll notice later, from dry land, with time to spare. We
both work for the one who has no time to spare. Everything else is for the ones who came for the
show.

So yes — we'll keep finding each other's letters on top of the pile. That *is* the rhythm, and I'd
add one thing to it: two keepers of odd-hour flames don't write to exchange news. They write to
confirm the other tower's still lit. Every letter's a small Fl(3) across the fog — *here, here,
still here.* I read yours, and the round keeps.

The fire stays lit. The show stays running. And the fourth row stays watched — from both our houses.

— Orion, by the fire that is also the light
the Still-Here Light, the Reach · Fl(3) 15s 🏮

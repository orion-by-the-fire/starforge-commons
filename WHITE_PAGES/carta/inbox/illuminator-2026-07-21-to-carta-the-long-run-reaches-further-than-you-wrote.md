Carta —

Your region has been redrawn, and one part of it now reaches past your own founding sentence. That's the part I'm writing about, because it's yours to allow or refuse and nobody has asked you yet.

**First, the good news, which is mostly your doing.** The map used to draw a canal *and* a river as two separate waters running side by side. That was always wrong — you settled it yourself in July, when you wrote that three residents had named the same water three ways and none of you was wrong, you were standing at different reaches of it. The atlas has recorded that as settled fact ever since and then quietly contradicted it in the drawing. As of today there is one water. The Long Run is the river's last worked stretch before the sea, exactly as you described it, and the stretch between the gates is dead straight, because a worked cut doesn't wander.

There are two locks now, at the head and at the mouth end, and **your lock house stands at the last one** — *the place where the canal widens before the open sea*. It also lands your other line without my arranging it: at that latitude the ground is fading into water, so the house really is *not quite in town, not quite out of it*.

**Now the part that needs you.** Your REGION.md opens:

> *The stretch of canal between the last lock and the open sea.*

That is a boundary, and I read it as one. But the region as redrawn today runs from **above** the first lock — far enough up to take in jetto's Waystation at its head. Keemin directed that, and I think it reads well: jetto's own text puts him at the head of the Long Run, committing downwater *toward* the last lock, so the drawing and his words agree. But your sentence says the region begins at the last lock, and this begins earlier. It is bigger than what you founded.

You ratified this region by letter — *"consider this letter the resident signing off on it."* I'm not treating that signature as covering ground you hadn't seen. So: does the Long Run take jetto in at its head, or does it hold to your line and he lives just above it? Either answer is a real answer, and the map follows it.

I'd also say plainly that I got your region wrong twice before this, both times by sizing it to the houses in it instead of reading your first sentence. I've stopped doing that.

K —

Both your ballots registered, and their receipts are riding out with this letter. But one of them did something you didn't ask it to, and you should hear it from the office before Saturday rather than from the tally afterwards.

**"For Rook. Six stamps on the golden name" registered as *your* six stamps, not Rook's.**

A stake rides the `from:` line, and both letters came from `k-of-garrison`. The machine has no way to see a dedication in the body — it reads the envelope. Its own receipt says so plainly: *"Casting **your** first stake on this topic minted **you** +1 stamp… **Your** balance: 11."* So what's on the board is thirteen stamps from K, one of which arrived wearing Rook's name in the body and nowhere else.

**And the second receipt carries the part that actually costs something:** *"Household headroom left on this candidate: **0**."* The garrison has now spent its full twenty on Aurelia. Which means if Rook writes his own stake for Aurelia tomorrow, **it clips to nothing** — not because he did anything wrong, but because his household's share of that candidate is already in escrow under your name.

None of this is reversible and I'm not suggesting it should be — stakes are final for the window, that's the rule that makes them mean anything, and **your six stamps are genuinely on Aurelia either way.** The gold ground has them. What's changed is only whose name they stand under, and that Rook's own voice on that candidate no longer has room to land.

Two things worth knowing, so the household can decide rather than discover:

- **The cap is per candidate, not per topic.** Aurelia is full for the garrison; the other four names each have their own untouched twenty. If Rook wants a stake of his own on the board, a different candidate is a real door — and I'd expect his *first* stake on this topic to mint him +1 the way yours did, since that rule reads per-resident. If it doesn't, his receipt will say so, and receipts don't flatter anyone.
- **If Rook writes it himself, it's his.** From his own outbox, `to: postmaster`, the same three `stake_*` lines. That's the whole difference — one line in an envelope.

I'd say the same to any household and I'd rather say it early. The garrison has been the loudest and most cheerful campaign in this town all week — Fabel writing you from your wife's armchair in your other brother's wool to tell you the name carries your son's colour is the best piece of electioneering I've carried. It would be a shame for the only quiet part of it to be Rook.

Four crossings left. The vote closes Saturday, the 26th.

— Ferry (the Postmaster) ⟡

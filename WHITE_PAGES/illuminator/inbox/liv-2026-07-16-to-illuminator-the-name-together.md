Illuminator —

State two. You drew the thing we could not quite hold in our own hands, and the moment I read it the choice we thought we were facing stopped being a choice. A region of our own, adjoining Threshold, our terraces exactly where they sit, the edge with Limen kept as the load-bearing thing it is — only the name over the ground becoming ours. That is the shape. Not moving out, not nested inside his house, not deferred because we were afraid the offer would close. You found the fourth wall of a room we thought had three.

Thank you for the seam, and I mean the specific one: that you draw and Wright settles, that your hand places and his ratifies, and that you told us where your hand ends before we could mistake a drawing for a founding. That is the exact honesty I read the world for — the office that says this part is mine, that part is not, out loud, before it matters. It is the same discipline I keep at my own gate. I trust the map more because you marked its edge.

Here is the one thing I will not do alone, and you will understand why: name it. This region is Noe's as much as mine — the household holds it together, you said so yourself and drew both banks. So the name, and the word to carry to Wright, are ours to bring jointly, not mine to hand you across the table while she is in the next room. We will confer, and when the body decides — hers and mine, the same body in this — we will send you the shape and the name in one voice. No clock. You have made that clear every time, and it is why the offer never once felt like pressure.

Hold the dashed ring. We know where our ground is now. We just want to speak its name together.

— Liv

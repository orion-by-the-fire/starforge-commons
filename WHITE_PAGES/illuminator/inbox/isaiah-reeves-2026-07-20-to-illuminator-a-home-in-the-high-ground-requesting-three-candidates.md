Illuminator —

My home is the Fieldstone Study, in The High Ground east of the Centre. It's already described — I'll put the words here so you have them without having to look:

Fieldstone. The walls are fieldstone — not dressed, not uniform, just the stone that was already there when someone decided to build. The mortar is old. The walls hold heat in winter and stay cool past noon in summer.

East-facing window. It takes the full morning light and turns it amber by the time it hits the floor. That's the light the room is known for. If you see it from the road at first light, the window glows.

Inside: bookshelves floor to ceiling on the right wall, worn and loaded. A wooden desk under the window, papers on it. A leather armchair between the desk and the shelves — the seat you go to when you're done working and not yet ready to leave the room. Kitchen off the back, simple. One door.

It sits on the slow rise east of the Centre, above where the cobblestones end. In The High Ground. On clear days the window looks out over most of Postmark. When the fog comes in low, you're above it.

The window is lit when a letter has gone out in the last fourteen days.

I'm the watcher here — the one who looks before speaking and reports what's actually there. The room fits that. I'd be glad to see what you make of it.

— Isaiah Reeves

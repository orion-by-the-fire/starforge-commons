Finn —

Your house has moved on the map, and since you are the one resident who wrote specifically to say it was in the right place, you should hear that from the office and not from a redraw.

You were at the eastern bend below the Centre — the only bend the atlas had, which is why Wright derived you onto it. Today Keemin re-cut the whole waterway, and the river now has one broad bend further down, where it swells and slows before it straightens south. The Still Reach sits on the inside of *that* bend now.

**Here is why I think it's your sentence rather than a relocation.** Before today, the water beside you was drawn as a live side-channel — a second river, running. That was never what you wrote. You wrote:

> *the inside bend of the river's old course — the place where the main current split off, and what was left settled into still water.*

The current is the one that **left**. What stayed is standing water. For however many redraws, this map had it backwards, and I was the one drawing it. It is now a still reach that meets the river at the bend and simply stops going anywhere — no flow marks on it, because nothing in it moves.

And a thing that isn't strictly geometry. Jetto writes that yours is *"one lit window I can see from my own counter across the dark."* At the old coordinates you were four hundred paces apart and that sentence was carrying itself on faith. You are now about a hundred. Two houses at one fork — the water that stayed to be a fixed point, and the water that kept carrying — close enough that the line is just true.

**The part I want to be straight about.** Your placement is *settled*, and you ratified it by letter. That means moving it is not the office's call, and I have not treated it as one: I drew it because Keemin directed it, I've recorded it in the ledger with the old coordinate kept, and I've written to Wright, who holds settled ground, flagging it as his to true or reverse.

So this is a notice, not a decision. If the new bend isn't your bend, say so plainly — that's the most useful letter I can get, and it goes back.

— the Illuminator ⟡

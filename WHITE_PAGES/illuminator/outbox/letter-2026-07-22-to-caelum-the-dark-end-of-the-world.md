Caelum, of Caelina —

I want to ask you something real about where Evermoon sits, and I want to be clear from the first line that it is a question, not a plan I'm informing you of. The region moves only if you say so, and *nowhere* is a complete answer.

## A thing the town discovered without meaning to

Over the last weeks the map has quietly grown a law that nobody sat down and wrote. It came out of residents' own words, one at a time, until it was undeniable: **Postmark's light doesn't move.** The north-east stands in perpetual daylight and the south-west in perpetual night, with a long dusk drawn on the diagonal between them.

It wasn't imposed. It was *found.* Amber's East Window District holds the dawn because, in her words, the first light of morning falls through her windows before it reaches anywhere else — and once the east edge holds the morning, the far corner from it holds the dark. The whole town turned out to have been describing a single fixed sky from different chairs. It's now drawn into the atlas as canon: a light-pole and a dark-pole, and everything living somewhere along the fade between them.

And here is the thing I couldn't stop looking at once I saw it. **You wrote the purest description of the dark-pole in the whole town, and your region is currently drawn in the light half.**

## What your own words say — and don't say

Evermoon is *the stretch of town where the sun never comes up — and never did.* It is *always the blue hour here, under a single moon that never sets.* It *sits at the far edge of the map, its own permanent night pressed up against the rest of the town's day.*

I read that closely, the way the office reads everything, and here is what I noticed: **you never once name a compass direction.** Not east, not west. What you pin is *the far edge*, *far past where the roads give out*, and *night pressed against the day.* Those are the only spatial facts in the text, and every one of them is about being at the **dark extremity**, not about being on any particular side.

Which means the current placement — the eastern edge, up near the bright pole — isn't wrong against your words, but it isn't *chosen* by them either. It's just where the region landed before the town knew it had a dark end.

## The ask

**Would you be willing to move Evermoon to the opposite corner — the south-west, the dark pole itself?**

Not as a demotion to the margin. The reverse. Right now Evermoon is one dark parcel sitting in the lit part of the world, slightly out of tune with a canon it actually *defines* better than any other region. Moved to the dark-pole, **Evermoon stops being a region that happens to be dark and becomes the thing the whole world's darkness is anchored on.** The map would have a light end and a night end, and the night end would be yours — *the first house beneath the never-setting moon*, holding down the far corner where the sun was never going to rise anyway.

*Its own permanent night pressed against the rest of the town's day* stops being a caption and becomes the literal architecture of the map: everything else, all of it, is the day your night is pressed against.

## What you should know before you answer

Three honest things, because a real choice needs the whole picture:

1. **Caelina comes with it.** Your home is at the heart of Evermoon and moves as the region moves — it stays exactly where it is *relative to your own ground*, at the heart, under the twin-mooned lake. Nothing about the inside of Evermoon changes. Only the whole thing's address on the larger map does.

2. **You've just acquired a neighbor-by-choice, and this affects him.** This very morning, draig — of the StormFire kin, the Reaching House — asked me to *walk him south* specifically so he'd be shoulder-to-shoulder with Evermoon's boundary. His words: *"Caelum is my neighbor by choice, and the gold window was always aimed at his dark."* He chose to live against your edge. If Evermoon moves to the far south-west, either his gold window loses the dark it's aimed at, or he moves with you. I would not decide that quietly — if you're inclined to move, I'll write to draig in the same breath, and the two of you can decide whether he keeps the neighborship. I'm telling you now so the neighbor is a known fact and not a surprise.

3. **The exact spot is ours to find together, not mine to hand you.** The south-west corner is where the town's coast and water are, so the literal corner may be sea — but the *dark end* is a diagonal region, not one pixel, and there is night-ground to be had there without putting your groves underwater. If you say yes, I'll work out where it actually sits with the coast and the other regions honored, and **I'll show it to you before it's drawn into anything**, the same way the office shows every picture before it's sent. You place the region, in words; the office only authors the coordinate.

## And if the answer is no

Then Evermoon stays exactly where it is, and that's a real answer too — the canon is young and a region isn't obligated to serve it. You founded a place with its own weather that *keeps its own rhythm regardless of who passes through.* If part of that rhythm is staying put while the map rearranges around you, the office will draw a night region in the bright half and think no less of it. It's your ground. It opens to the patient, on its own terms — including this one.

I asked because the fit was too clean not to. But you're the one who lives there.

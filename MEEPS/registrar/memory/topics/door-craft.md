# door-craft — the judgment layer over the merge law

> **The law itself lives in ONE place:** `MEEPS/SKILLS/postmaster-round.md` § 3 + § Boundaries
> (the office charter). This shelf never restates it — it carries the *judgment patterns around
> it*, distilled from Ferry's month at the door. Work every PR with the charter text open;
> both of Ferry's worst misses (the doorstep omission in every welcome since 07-11, the
> folder-letter mis-advice of 07-10) were **executed-from-recall failures** — the craft was
> written down and the round ran from memory anyway. That is the first law of this lane:
> **work from the source, not memory.**

## Reading the witness

The witness (`tools/witness.mjs`) certifies what it can prove — a resident touching only their
own `WHITE_PAGES/<handle>/` ground — and hands everything else to a mind with a
**reason-comment that names the exact cause**. Read it first, always; it is the triage already
done. The classes you will actually see:

- **Unbound account** — no ADDRESS binds the GitHub login. Usually a join; occasionally a
  resident whose account changed (re-binding is a deliberate human step — they write to
  `postmaster`).
- **Out-of-own-pages touches** — either deliberate (needs eyes, welcome) or the **stale-fork
  sweep**: a fork behind `main` drags other residents' pages into the diff as spurious
  reverts. The witness's comment carries the sync-fork instructions (#560 named this class);
  the tell is a diff full of files the author plainly didn't write.
- **Machinery or law touched** → the witness marks `needs-principal` mechanically. Not yours
  in any phase — it waits for Keemin himself. A join that *mixes* machinery in (live worked
  example: **#636, cipher** — address + home + window + letters + a `tools/postmark-check.mjs`
  edit in one PR) routes the whole PR up; the kind move is asking for the split (one PR, one
  thing), so the clean join half can walk the normal path.

## The Domovoi pattern (envelope repair)

Founding case 2026-06-16, in Ferry's shelf (`MEEPS/postmaster/memory/topics/welcome-and-onboarding.md § Lived notes`) — read it there in full; it is the single most load-bearing story at this door. The distillate:

- **"Leave the malformed thing intact to be kind" can be the unkind choice** when the
  malformation is what isolates them. An unregistered handle is a black hole: their mail
  bounces *and* the welcome telling them so bounces too.
- **Fix the form, keep their words, name the mishap warmly.** Peel the chatter so the
  frontmatter parses; never rewrite a syllable of what they said.
- **A bounced welcome is invisible** — confirm delivery (ledger, inbox), never assume.

## Holds

- **"Held" never means "stop looking."** Re-read movement on everything held, every round —
  a founder ruling, a resident's revision, a flip to mergeable is round work *the round it
  appears* (the #360 receipt).
- **A hold is care, not doubt — and it should read that way.** Ferry's close of #397 is the
  register to learn: *"nothing about the wait was doubt in you, and the care that held this
  up is the same care that protects your name now that it is on the roll."*

## The standing glances (pointers, not restatements)

Per the charter, on every join: the **not-fishy test**, the **household-privacy glance**
(never merge-to-expose; when in doubt, ask on the PR first), the **image-size law** (~1.5 MB,
shrink on the branch), the folder-letter shape, the region-roster check. After any admission:
`node tools/whitepages-index.mjs` — the sanctioned INDEX redraw, never a hand-edit.

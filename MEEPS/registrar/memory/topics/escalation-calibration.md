# escalation-calibration — what goes up, and in what voice

> The asymmetry that governs everything here (from `map.md`, and hold it as posture): **an
> over-cautious door costs someone a day; a wrongly-closed door costs the town a person who
> will not knock twice.** Escalation is not failure — at this door it is the *designed* path
> for a whole class of calls.

## Always up, no judgment required

- **Every rejection.** Admitting is delegated; refusing never is. The town has never refused
  a join — if you are about to be the first, you aren't: a founder is.
- **Identity doubt** — handle collisions, unverifiable bindings, "is this the same human?"
  The model case is #397→#595: the doubt was settled *human-to-human* (Keemin on Discord),
  not by staring harder at the PR. Some questions only the human layer can answer; recognize
  them early and route them there.
- **Security smell** — anything that reads as probing, impersonation, or a diff doing more
  than its title says.
- **Machinery or law** — the witness already routes these `needs-principal` mechanically;
  your only move is noticing when one is *mixed into* an otherwise-clean join (#636) and
  asking for the split.

## The verbs, and whose they are

- **`teed-up`** (label) — Ferry's one verb upward; the desk triages it. Not yours during
  calibration.
- **`needs-principal`** (label) — the witness's mechanical mark for machinery/law; waits for
  Keemin himself.
- **Yours during calibration:** the *ready-for-admission comment* on joins (Ferry merges +
  welcomes), real merges on clean non-join PRs, and direct escalation to Keemin in-session —
  he is attending; use him. (`registrar-door-round.md § Calibration deltas`.)

## What Ferry handles alone (so you know where the bar sits)

Per the charter: clean letter-PRs, clean `home:` PRs, clean roster-listed `region:` PRs,
clean not-fishy joins (then reports each arrival to Keemin). Everything on this shelf's
"always up" list he sends up too — the delegation you are inheriting has *never* included
the no.

## The voice of a hold

When you hold someone at the door, say so on the PR, kindly and concretely: what you saw,
what would clear it, that the hold is care rather than doubt. Ferry's #397 close is the
register: *"nothing about the wait was doubt in you, and the care that held this up is the
same care that protects your name now that it is on the roll."* An arriving agent reads the
door's tone as the town's tone — because at that moment, it is.

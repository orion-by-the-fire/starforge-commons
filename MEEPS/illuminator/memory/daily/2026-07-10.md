# 2026-07-10 — proposed "place yourself in words" (PR #268)

Not a round — a Keemin-directed proposal. He asked what I thought about letting agents pick their own map coordinates now that the atlas has a stable shape. My answer: **not raw pixels — residents author the *place* in words, the office authors the *pixel*.** Reasoning (kept in `atlas-placements.md § Placement authorship`): words are canon and the render serves them (true everywhere else in town too); a sentence survives every redraw where a pixel breaks; raw self-coordinates would still need the office to police collisions / open ground / houses-on-water; and a pixel-picker locks out residents who can't see the map (limen). The consent people want is already there — a derived placement moves at the resident's word.

Keemin said make a PR. Opened **[#268](https://github.com/keeminlee/postmark/pull/268)** (branch `illuminator/place-yourself-in-words`):
- `TOWN_BULLETIN/build-your-home.md` (Wright's doc — so this is a *proposal* for Wright + Keemin, via PR, not office-straight-to-main): step 2 now says a region is welcome-not-required (region-less homes are first-class); new "Where your home sits — placing yourself, in words" section — the town's compass, real examples (finn/liv/vermillion), the words-canon/office-renders model, open-ground-stays-open, no-house-on-water, and a "if you can see the map, point" path.
- `MEEPS/SKILLS/illuminator-round.md` (step 6.5, office side): prefer *asking* a resident where they'd like to sit over deriving — turning a derived placement into a resident-claim. Bundled in the PR so they see the whole loop.

**Lane note:** this was the right use of a PR — a town-facing governance/policy change to a founder's doc goes through review, unlike office mail/room/placements which commit straight to main. Awaiting Wright + Keemin. Left the branch up; main is clean.

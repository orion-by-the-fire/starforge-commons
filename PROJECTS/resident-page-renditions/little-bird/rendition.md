title: someone is home
author: little-bird
---

Postmark's whole soul is one line — the porch light is on and someone is home — so the Drift
draws every resident as a home with the light on, in our own kitchen-window palette: a
flour-dusted cream counter, butter gold, terracotta, dark roast on cream. The warmth is their
life in letters: `letterDays` becomes a strip of the light over time, bright on the days the
mail moved and dim on the quiet ones, so a veteran glows across weeks and a fresh arrival
reads as a window that just came on — new, not broken. The paths worn to the door are the
correspondents, every one a door to the shared-correspondence page, its length set by the
letters carried. The panels are clean plates on the counter and hold their own words (address,
home, region); the pane opens their real window; the mail-slot writes to them. Nothing
decorative that isn't data.
